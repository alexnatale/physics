Homework 6 Solutions: Work & Energy

Posted: Tuesday, June 3 2025

Problem 1 – Basic Work Calculation

  1. Work by applied force: \(W = Fd\cos\theta = 75.0\,\text{N}\times12.0\,\text{m}\times\cos25^{\circ} = 8.16\times10^{2}\,\text{J}\)
  2. Work by friction: \(W_f = -\mu_k\,N\,d = -0.15\!\left(mg+F\sin25^{\circ}\right)\!d = -6.74\times10^{2}\,\text{J}\)
  3. Net work: \(W_{\text{net}} = W + W_f = 1.41\times10^{2}\,\text{J}\)

Problem 2 – Work–Energy Theorem

  1. \(\Delta K = \tfrac12 m v^{2} = 0.5(1500)(25.0)^{2} = 4.69\times10^{5}\,\text{J}\)
  2. Work done (equals \(\Delta K\)): 4.69 × 105 J
  3. \(F_{\text{avg}} = \dfrac{W}{d} = \dfrac{4.69\times10^{5}\,\text{J}}{120\,\text{m}} = 3.91\times10^{3}\,\text{N}\)

Problem 3 – Power Calculations

  1. \(W = mgh = (65.0)(9.8)(4.5) = 2.87\times10^{3}\,\text{J}\)
  2. \(P = \dfrac{W}{t} = \dfrac{2.87\times10^{3}\,\text{J}}{6.0\,\text{s}} = 4.78\times10^{2}\,\text{W}\)
  3. \(P = \dfrac{4.78\times10^{2}\,\text{W}}{746\,\text{W/hp}} \approx 0.64\,\text{hp}\)
  4. \(\text{Efficiency} = \dfrac{P_{\text{actual}}}{P} = \dfrac{380}{478}\approx79.5\%\)

Problem 4 – Gravitational Potential Energy

  1. \(K_i = 0.5(2.0)(15.0)^{2} = 225\,\text{J}\)
  2. \(U_i = mgh = (2.0)(9.8)(1.2) = 23.5\,\text{J}\)
  3. \(h_{\max} = \dfrac{K_i+U_i}{mg} \approx 12.7\,\text{m}\)
  4. \(v = \sqrt{\dfrac{2(K_i+U_i)}{m}} \approx 15.8\,\text{m/s}\)

Problem 5 – Elastic Potential Energy

  1. \(U = \tfrac12 kx^{2} = 0.5(200)(0.150)^{2} = 2.25\,\text{J}\)
  2. \(v = \sqrt{\dfrac{2U}{m}} = 3.00\,\text{m/s}\)
  3. \(h = \dfrac{U}{mg} \approx 0.46\,\text{m}\)

Problem 6 – Energy with Non-Conservative Forces

  1. \(W_g = mgd\sin\theta = 73.5\,\text{J}\)
  2. \(W_f = -\mu_k mgd\cos\theta = -31.8\,\text{J}\)
  3. \(v = \sqrt{\dfrac{2(W_g+W_f)}{m}} = 4.08\,\text{m/s}\)
  4. Thermal energy generated: 31.8 J

Problem 7 – Conservation of Mechanical Energy

  1. \(U_i = mgL(1-\cos\theta) = 2.63\,\text{J}\)
  2. \(v = \sqrt{\dfrac{2U_i}{m}} = 2.29\,\text{m/s}\)
  3. Ideal return angle: 30°
  4. With 8 % loss: ≈ 29°

Problem 8 – Energy Conversion (Roller Coaster)

  1. \(U_i = mgh = 1.41\times10^{5}\,\text{J}\)
  2. Thermal energy lost: 5.25 × 104 J
  3. Percentage lost: 37 %

Problem 9 – Potential Energy & Stability

  1. Equilibrium: x = 3 m
  2. Nature: Stable (\(d^{2}U/dx^{2}=4\gt0\))
  3. Allowed positions for \(E_{\text{tot}}=0\): only x = 3 m
  4. Maximum speed at \(E_{\text{tot}}=0\): 0 m/s

Problem 10 – Energy Conversion Efficiency

  1. Incident power: 8.0 kW
  2. Electrical output (18 %): 1.44 kW
  3. Height a 50 kg mass can be lifted in 10 s: 29.4 m
  4. Overall efficiency: 8.1 %
