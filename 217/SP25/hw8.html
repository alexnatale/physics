Homework 8: Angular Momentum & Rotational Motion

Due: Tuesday, May 27, 2025, 11:59 PM

Problem 1 (Rolling Cylinder): A solid cylinder (\(m=4.0\,\text{kg}\), \(r=0.5\,\text{m}\)) rolls without slipping at 6.0 m/s.
(a) Find its angular speed about its center (\(\omega = v/r\)).
(b) Compute its total angular momentum \(L\) about its center, using \(I\) for a solid cylinder.
(c) Compare its translational and rotational kinetic energies.

Problem 2 (Basic Angular Momentum): A disc rotating at 10.0 rad/s has a moment of inertia \(2.5\,\text{kg}\cdot\text{m}^2\).
(a) Calculate its angular momentum.
(b) If a torque of 5.0 N·m is applied slowing it down, how long before it stops?

Problem 3 (Figure Skater & I Change): A figure skater reduces her moment of inertia from 4.0 to 2.5 kg·m\(^2\) by pulling in her arms. If her initial angular speed was 3.0 rad/s,
(a) Use conservation of angular momentum to find her new angular speed.
(b) By what factor does her rotational kinetic energy change?

Problem 4 (Uniform Angular Acceleration): A wheel accelerates uniformly from rest to 15.0 rad/s in 4.0 s.
(a) Calculate its angular acceleration.
(b) Find the total angle rotated in that time.
(c) If its moment of inertia is 1.2 kg·m\(^2\), what net torque is required?

Problem 5 (Rotational KE of a Disc): A disc (\(m=3.0\,\text{kg}, r=0.4\,\text{m}\)) rotates at 8.0 rad/s.
(a) Calculate its rotational kinetic energy \(\bigl(K = \tfrac{1}{2}I\omega^2,\;I=\tfrac{1}{2}mr^2\bigr)\).
(b) If friction exerts a constant torque of 0.50 N·m, how many revolutions occur before it stops?

Problem 6 (Relationship \(L = I\omega\)): A rotating object has moment of inertia \(5.0\,\text{kg}\cdot\text{m}^2\) and an angular momentum \(20.0\,\text{kg}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{s}\).
(a) Use \(L = I\omega\) to find its angular speed.
(b) If the object slows to half this angular speed, by what factor does its angular momentum change?

Problem 7 (Spool Unwinding): A spool (mass = 1.0 kg, radius = 0.1 m) unwinds under gravity as a rope is pulled from above. Assume the tension \(T\) is constant and directed vertically upward at the spool’s rim.
(a) Write the net torque on the spool about its center.
(b) Derive the angular acceleration \(\alpha\) in terms of \(T, m, g,\) and \(R\).
(c) If \(T\) is half the weight \(mg\), find \(\alpha\) and the linear acceleration of the spool’s center of mass.

Problem 8 (Bicycle Wheel): A bicycle wheel rotates at 12.0 rad/s and has a moment of inertia 0.8 kg·m\(^2\).
(a) Calculate its angular momentum.
(b) If a brake applies a constant torque of 1.2 N·m, how long until the wheel stops?

Problem 9 (Angular Acceleration & Rotation): A rotating platform with \(I = 10\,\text{kg}\cdot\text{m}^2\) experiences a constant angular acceleration of \(0.5\,\text{rad/s}^2\).
(a) How much torque is required?
(b) Through what angle does it rotate in 6.0 s, starting from rest?
(c) What is its final angular velocity?

Problem 10 (Rod about Center): A uniform rod (length = 1.5 m, mass = 2.0 kg) rotates about its center at 4.0 rad/s.
(a) Use integration or known formulas to find \(I\) for a uniform rod about its center.
(b) Determine its angular momentum \(L = I\omega\).
(c) How would \(L\) change if the rod rotated about one end at the same \(\omega\)?
