Homework 1: Electromagnetic Induction and Inductance

Due: Friday, May 30, 2025, 11:59 PM

Problem 1 (Faraday’s and Lenz’s Law): A circular loop of radius R = 0.10 m sits in a uniform magnetic field directed into the page, with dB/dt = 0.20 T/s.
(a) Using Lenz’s law, state whether the induced current is clockwise or counterclockwise as viewed from above.
(b) Calculate the magnitude of the induced emf, ε = πR²·(dB/dt).
(c) If the loop has resistance 5.0 Ω, determine the induced current.

Problem 2 (Motional emf in a sliding rod): A conducting rod of length ℓ = 0.30 m slides without friction on two parallel rails separated by d = 0.25 m. The rails are connected at one end by a resistor R = 2.0 Ω, forming a closed loop. A uniform magnetic field B = 0.50 T is directed into the plane. The rod moves to the right at constant velocity v = 5.0 m/s.
(a) Calculate the motional emf, ε = B·d·v.
(b) Determine the induced current in the circuit.
(c) Compute the magnetic force on the rod and state its direction.

Problem 3 (Induced electric fields): Assume the solenoid’s radius exceeds 0.15 m so that the circular loop (r = 0.15 m) lies entirely within its windings.
A long solenoid with n = 500 turns/m carries a current that decreases uniformly from 4.0 A to 0 A in 0.10 s. A coaxial circular loop of radius r = 0.15 m surrounds it.
(a) Calculate the initial magnetic flux through the loop.
(b) Calculate the magnitude of the induced emf in the loop.
(c) Determine the magnitude of the induced electric field at radius r.

Problem 4 (Eddy currents and energy dissipation): A thick, nonmagnetic metal plate falls at constant speed through a uniform magnetic field region. Induced eddy currents dissipate mechanical energy as heat.
(a) Explain why the plate reaches a terminal velocity.
(b) Identify the principle relating lost mechanical energy to induced currents.
(c) Discuss how changing the plate’s electrical conductivity affects its terminal speed.

Problem 5 (Back emf in electric generators): A single‐loop generator has N = 100 turns, loop area A = 0.020 m², and rotates at ω = 50 rad/s in a uniform field B = 0.10 T. The loop circuit has resistance R = 2.0 Ω.
(a) Calculate the amplitude of the induced emf.
(b) If connected to the resistive load, determine the steady‐state current.
(c) Explain qualitatively how increasing ω affects both the emf amplitude and load current.

Problem 6 (Mutual inductance in coupled coils): Two tightly coupled coils have mutual inductance M = 0.020 H. Coil 1’s current changes at dI₁/dt = 10 A/s. Coil 2 has resistance R₂ = 5.0 Ω.
(a) Calculate the induced emf in coil 2.
(b) Determine the induced current in coil 2 and its direction relative to ΔI₁.
(c) Explain how partial separation of the coils affects the induced emf.

Problem 7 (RL circuit time response): A series RL circuit has R = 10 Ω and L = 2.0 H connected to a DC source V = 20 V.
(a) What is the time constant τ for this circuit?
(b) How long does it take for the current to reach 90% of its final value?

Problem 8 (Energy stored in magnetic fields): An inductor of L = 0.50 H carries a current I = 3.0 A.
(a) Calculate the energy stored in the inductor.
(b) If this energy is released uniformly over 0.10 s, what power is delivered?
(c) For a solenoid ℓ = 0.50 m, cross-sectional area A = 1.0×10⁻³ m², and turn density n = 1000 turns/m carrying I = 3.0 A, compute the energy density in the magnetic field.

Problem 9 (Rotating coil generator): A rectangular coil of width w = 0.10 m and height h = 0.20 m rotates about its left vertical edge at ω = 100 rad/s in a uniform magnetic field B directed perpendicular to the coil’s plane at θ = 0.
(a) Calculate the amplitude of the induced emf.
(b) Determine the maximum current when the coil is connected to R = 5.0 Ω.
(c) Describe how back emf alters the mechanical torque required.

Problem 10 (Real-world application – induction cooktop): Induction cooktops heat cookware via eddy currents generated by changing magnetic fields.
(a) Explain how eddy currents produce heat in the cookware.
(b) Discuss why the cookware must be ferromagnetic for efficient heating.
(c) Identify one safety feature that protects users from stray fields.
