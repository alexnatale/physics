Homework 7: Rotational Motion and Dynamics (Ch. 10)

Due: Monday, May 19, 2025, 11:59 PM

Problem 1 (Angular Kinematics – easy): A turntable starts from rest and reaches 45 rev min‑1 in 6.0 s with constant angular acceleration.
(a) Determine its angular acceleration α in rad s‑2.
(b) Find the angular displacement, in radians, during the 6.0 s interval.
(c) How many complete revolutions does the turntable make in this time?
(d) What is its angular speed at \(t = 4.0\text{ s}\)?

Problem 2 (Conceptual – Relating Angular & Translational Quantities): A bicycle wheel of radius 0.35 m rolls without slipping at 7.0 m s‑1.
(a) Calculate its angular speed.
(b) What is the linear speed of a point on the rim at the top of the wheel relative to the ground?
(c) What is the speed of the axle relative to the ground?
(d) Briefly explain the physical reason for the answers to parts (b) and (c).

Problem 3 (Moment of Inertia & Rotational KE – medium): A uniform solid disk of mass 4.0 kg and radius 0.20 m spins at 12 rad s‑1.
(a) Compute its moment of inertia about the central axis.
(b) Determine its rotational kinetic energy.
(c) If it is accelerated to 20 rad s‑1, how much additional kinetic energy is required?
(d) How long must a constant 1.2 N·m torque act to achieve the speed change of part (c)?

Problem 4 (Composite Moment of Inertia – hard): A 0.50 kg thin rod, 0.60 m long, has a 0.20 kg point mass fastened to one end.
(a) Find the moment of inertia of the system about an axis through the opposite end of the rod, perpendicular to its length.
(b) If the system rotates at 5.0 rad s‑1 about this axis, compute its rotational kinetic energy.
(c) Determine the moment of inertia about an axis through the composite center of mass, perpendicular to the rod.

Problem 5 (Torque & Angular Acceleration – medium, real‑world): A playground merry‑go‑round is a solid cylinder of radius 2.0 m and mass 250 kg. A child pushes tangentially with a 100 N force for 4.0 s, starting from rest.
(a) Determine the torque exerted on the merry‑go‑round.
(b) Calculate its angular acceleration.
(c) Find its angular speed at \(t = 4.0\text{ s}\).
(d) Compute the work done by the child’s push.

Problem 6 (Newton’s 2nd Law for Rotation – hard): Two masses, 3.0 kg and 2.0 kg, are connected by a light string over a pulley of radius 0.15 m and moment of inertia 0.040 kg·m². The string does not slip.
(a) Derive expressions and determine the system’s linear acceleration.
(b) Find the tension in each segment of the string.
(c) Determine the angular acceleration of the pulley.
(d) After the heavier mass has descended 1.2 m, what is the pulley’s angular speed?

Problem 7 (Work & Power in Rotational Motion – medium): A drill’s motor supplies a steady 0.20 N·m torque to a chuck‑and‑bit assembly (treated as a uniform cylinder, mass 1.5 kg, radius 0.050 m) that starts from rest.
(a) Calculate the rotational kinetic energy after 2.0 s.
(b) Find the angular speed at that instant.
(c) Determine the power output of the motor at \(t = 2.0\text{ s}\).
(d) If the drill encounters wood that exerts a resistive torque of 0.20 N·m, what constant angular speed will the bit eventually reach?

Problem 8 (Rolling Without Slipping – medium): A solid sphere (mass 2.5 kg, radius 0.12 m) starts from rest at the top of a 1.8 m‑high incline 5.0 m long.
(a) Determine its translational speed at the bottom, assuming pure rolling.
(b) Calculate its angular speed there.
(c) What minimum coefficient of static friction is required to prevent slipping on the incline?

Problem 9 (Rotational Work & Door Motion – easy‑medium): A 0.90 m‑wide, 18 kg door (modeled as a uniform thin sheet) is pushed perpendicular to the surface with a 12 N force at its outer edge through an angle of 65°.
(a) How much work does the force do on the door?
(b) What is the resulting change in rotational kinetic energy of the door?
(c) Assuming the door starts from rest, find its angular speed after the push.

Problem 10 (Braking & Power Dissipation – hard, multi‑concept): A 1200 kg car moving at 20 m s‑1 has four identical wheels, each a solid cylinder of mass 15 kg and radius 0.30 m. The brakes apply 45 N·m of torque to each wheel.
(a) Find the angular deceleration of a wheel.
(b) How long does it take the car to stop (no skidding, wheels roll without slipping)?
(c) How far does the car travel while stopping?
(d) Determine the average power dissipated by one brake during the stop.
