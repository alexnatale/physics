(Measurement & Units)

Problem 1: Multi-Part Conversions

Given: \(1.00\,\text{in} = 2.54\,\text{cm}\) and \(1\,\text{mile} = 1.609\,\text{km}\).

  1. (a) Convert \(6.739\) miles to kilometers:
    • Calculation:
      \(6.739 \, \text{miles} \times 1.609\,\frac{\text{km}}{\text{mile}} \approx 10.84\,\text{km}\)
  2. (b) Convert \(1.00\,\text{km}\) to feet:
    • \(1.00\,\text{km} = 1000\,\text{m}\)
    • \(1\,\text{m} = 3.28084\,\text{ft}\)
    • \(1000\,\text{m} \times 3.28084\,\frac{\text{ft}}{\text{m}} \approx 3280.84\,\text{ft}\)
  3. (c) Convert \(2.00\) miles to centimeters:
    • Step 1: Convert miles to kilometers:
      \(2.00 \, \text{miles} \times 1.609\,\frac{\text{km}}{\text{mile}} = 3.218\,\text{km}\)
    • Step 2: Convert kilometers to meters:
      \(3.218\,\text{km} \times 1000\,\frac{\text{m}}{\text{km}} = 3218\,\text{m}\)
    • Step 3: Convert meters to centimeters:
      \(3218\,\text{m} \times 100\,\frac{\text{cm}}{\text{m}} = 321800\,\text{cm}\)

Problem 2: Area Conversions & Cost

  1. (a) Convert the field’s area into square feet:
    • \(120.0\,\text{m} \times 80.0\,\text{m} = 9600\,\text{m}^2\)
    • \(1\,\text{m}^2 = (3.28084\,\text{ft})^2 \approx 10.7639\,\text{ft}^2\)
    • \(9600\,\text{m}^2 \times 10.7639\,\frac{\text{ft}^2}{\text{m}^2} \approx 103416\,\text{ft}^2\)
  2. (b) Convert the area into acres:
    • \(1\,\text{acre} = 4047\,\text{m}^2\)
    • \(9600\,\text{m}^2 / 4047\,\text{m}^2 \approx 2.37\,\text{acres}\)
  3. (c) Determine the perimeter (in meters) then convert to feet:
    • \(2 \times (120.0\,\text{m} + 80.0\,\text{m}) = 2 \times 200.0\,\text{m} = 400.0\,\text{m}\)
    • \(400.0\,\text{m} \times 3.28084\,\frac{\text{ft}}{\text{m}} \approx 1312.34\,\text{ft}\)
  4. (d) Fencing cost:
    • \(1312.34\,\text{ft} \times \$3.50/\text{ft} \approx \$4593.19\)

Problem 3: Percent Uncertainty & Dimensional Analysis

  1. (a) Percent uncertainty for a rod measured as \(1.250\,\text{m} \pm 0.005\,\text{m}\):
    • \(\frac{0.005}{1.250} \times 100 \approx 0.4\%\)
  2. (b) Propagating the uncertainty for 20 such rods:
    • Total length \(= 20 \times 1.250\,\text{m} = 25.00\,\text{m}\)
    • If the error is systematic, total uncertainty \(= 20 \times 0.005\,\text{m} = 0.100\,\text{m}\).
      \(\frac{0.100}{25.00} \times 100 = 0.4\%\)
    • (Note: For independent random errors, the uncertainties add in quadrature.)

Problem 4: Energy Units

  1. Convert \(5.0\,\text{kJ}\) to calories:
    • \(5.0\,\text{kJ} = 5000\,\text{J}\)
    • \(1\,\text{cal} \approx 4.184\,\text{J}\), so
      \(5000\,\text{J} \div 4.184\,\text{J/cal} \approx 1195\,\text{cal}\)
  2. Convert to dietary “Calories”:
    • \(1\,\text{Calorie (dietary)} = 1000\,\text{cal}\)
    • \(1195\,\text{cal} \approx 1.20\,\text{Calories}\)

Problem 5: Mixed Conversions

  1. (a) Convert \(60.0\) miles to kilometers:
    • \(60.0\,\text{miles} \times 1.609\,\frac{\text{km}}{\text{mile}} = 96.54\,\text{km}\)
  2. (b) Convert \(2.5\) gallons to liters:
    • \(2.5\,\text{gallons} \times 3.785\,\frac{\text{L}}{\text{gallon}} \approx 9.46\,\text{L}\)
  3. (c) Compute fuel consumption in L per 100 km:
    • \(\frac{9.46\,\text{L}}{96.54\,\text{km}} \times 100 \approx 9.80\,\frac{\text{L}}{100\,\text{km}}\)

Problem 6: Density Conversions

  1. (a) Express \(2.70\,\text{g/cm}^3\) in kg/m³:
    • \(1\,\text{g/cm}^3 = 1000\,\text{kg/m}^3\).
      \(2.70\,\text{g/cm}^3 = 2.70 \times 1000 = 2700\,\text{kg/m}^3\)
  2. (b) Mass of a \(3.0\,\text{cm} \times 3.0\,\text{cm} \times 3.0\,\text{cm}\) cube:
    • Volume \(= 3.0^3 = 27.0\,\text{cm}^3\)
    • Mass \(= 2.70\,\text{g/cm}^3 \times 27.0\,\text{cm}^3 = 72.9\,\text{g}\)
    • Convert to kg: \(72.9\,\text{g} = 0.0729\,\text{kg}\)

Problem 7: Speed Conversion

  1. (a) Convert \(25.0\,\text{m/s}\) to km/h:
    • \(25.0\,\text{m/s} \times 3.6 = 90.0\,\text{km/h}\)
  2. (b) Convert \(25.0\,\text{m/s}\) to mph:
    • \(90.0\,\text{km/h} \div 1.609 \approx 55.9\,\text{mph}\)
  3. (c) Explain the ratio:
    • The conversion factor \(1\,\text{mile} \approx 1.609\,\text{km}\) implies a ratio of \(\frac{1}{1.609} \approx 0.621\). Hence \(55.9\,\text{mph}\) vs. \(90.0\,\text{km/h}.\)

Problem 8: Pressure Conversion

Problem 9: Time Units

  1. (a) Convert \(3.45\times10^{-2}\,\text{s}\) to milliseconds:
    • \(1\,\text{ms} = 10^{-3}\,\text{s}\).
      \(3.45\times10^{-2}\,\text{s} = 34.5\,\text{ms}\)
  2. (b) Convert to microseconds:
    • \(1\,\mu\text{s} = 10^{-6}\,\text{s}\).
      \(3.45\times10^{-2}\,\text{s} = 34500\,\mu\text{s}\)
  3. (c) Convert to nanoseconds:
    • \(1\,\text{ns} = 10^{-9}\,\text{s}\).
      \(3.45\times10^{-2}\,\text{s} = 34,500,000\,\text{ns}\)
  4. (d) Practicality:
    • For many lab measurements, milliseconds or microseconds are more typical than nanoseconds.

Problem 10: Dimensional Consistency & Short Derivation

  1. Verify \(KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2\) is dimensionally consistent:
    • Mass \(m\) has units of kg.
    • Velocity \(v\) has units of m/s; hence \(v^2\) is \(\text{m}^2/\text{s}^2\).
    • So \(KE\) has units of kg·m²/s² (joules).
  2. Calculation for a \(1.0\,\text{kg}\) mass moving at \(1.0\,\text{m/s}\):
    • \(KE = \tfrac12 \times 1.0\,\text{kg} \times (1.0\,\text{m/s})^2 = 0.5\,\text{J}\)
