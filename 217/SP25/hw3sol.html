Phys 217 Homework 3 Solutions
(Projectile Motion & Motion in 2 and 3 Dimensions)

Problem 1: Classic Projectile Variation

  1. (a) Time of Flight, Maximum Height, and Horizontal Range:
    • Given \(v_0 = 18.0\,\text{m/s}\) and \(\theta = 35^\circ\), the initial components are:
      \(v_{0x} = 18.0\cos35^\circ\) and \(v_{0y} = 18.0\sin35^\circ\).
    • Time of flight: \[ T = \frac{2v_{0y}}{g} = \frac{2(18.0\sin35^\circ)}{9.8} \approx \frac{2(10.32)}{9.8} \approx 2.11\,\text{s}. \]
    • Maximum height: \[ H = \frac{v_{0y}^2}{2g} \approx \frac{(10.32)^2}{19.6} \approx 5.44\,\text{m}. \]
    • Horizontal range: \[ R = v_{0x}T = (18.0\cos35^\circ)(2.11) \approx (18.0\times0.8192)(2.11) \approx 31.07\,\text{m}. \]
  2. (b) Constant Horizontal Velocity Explanation:
    • In the absence of air resistance there is no horizontal acceleration (\(a_x = 0\)), so the horizontal component of velocity remains constant.

Problem 2: Horizontal Launch Variation

  1. (a) Time Until Impact:
    • For a drop from \(20.0\,\text{m}\): \[ y = \frac{1}{2}gt^2 \quad\Rightarrow\quad t = \sqrt{\frac{2y}{g}} = \sqrt{\frac{2(20.0)}{9.8}} \approx 2.02\,\text{s}. \]
  2. (b) Horizontal Distance Traveled:
    • With \(v_x = 6.0\,\text{m/s}\): \[ x = v_x t \approx 6.0 \times 2.02 \approx 12.12\,\text{m}. \]
  3. (c) Velocity Just Before Impact:
    • Final vertical speed: \[ v_y = -9.8 \times 2.02 \approx -19.8\,\text{m/s}. \]
    • Resultant speed: \[ v = \sqrt{(6.0)^2 + (19.8)^2} \approx \sqrt{36 + 392.04} \approx 20.7\,\text{m/s}. \]
    • The direction is given by: \[ \theta = \arctan\left(\frac{19.8}{6.0}\right) \approx 73^\circ \text{ (below the horizontal)}. \]

Problem 3: Initial Velocity Components

  1. (a) Horizontal and Vertical Components:
    • For \(v_0 = 30.0\,\text{m/s}\) at \(\theta = 50^\circ\): \[ v_{0x} = 30.0\cos50^\circ \approx 19.28\,\text{m/s}, \quad v_{0y} = 30.0\sin50^\circ \approx 22.98\,\text{m/s}. \]
  2. (b) Free-Body Diagram:
    • The only force acting on the projectile (ignoring air resistance) is gravity (\(mg\)), which points downward.
  3. (c) Time to Return to Ground:
    • Time of flight: \[ T = \frac{2v_{0y}}{g} = \frac{2(22.98)}{9.8} \approx 4.69\,\text{s}. \]

Problem 4: Velocity at a Specific Time

  1. (a) Parametric Equations of Motion:
    • For an object launched with \(v_0 = 12.0\,\text{m/s}\) at \(\theta = 70^\circ\): \[ x(t) = 12.0\cos70^\circ\,t,\quad y(t) = 12.0\sin70^\circ\,t - 4.9t^2. \]
  2. (b) Velocity Vector at \(t = 1.5\,\text{s}\):
    • The horizontal velocity remains constant: \[ v_x = 12.0\cos70^\circ \approx 4.10\,\text{m/s}. \]
    • Vertical velocity: \[ v_y = 12.0\sin70^\circ - 9.8(1.5) \approx 11.28 - 14.7 \approx -3.42\,\text{m/s}. \]
    • Thus, the velocity vector is approximately \((4.10\,\hat{i},\,-3.42\,\hat{j})\,\text{m/s}\).
  3. (c) Speed Comparison:
    • The speed at \(t = 1.5\,\text{s}\) is \[ |\mathbf{v}| \approx \sqrt{(4.10)^2 + (-3.42)^2} \approx 5.35\,\text{m/s}, \] which is lower than the initial speed of \(12.0\,\text{m/s}\) due to the effect of gravity.

Problem 5: Launch from an Elevated Platform

  1. (a) Total Time in the Air:
    • With \(y_0 = 10.0\,\text{m}\), \(v_0 = 20.0\,\text{m/s}\), and \(\theta = 25^\circ\):
    • Components: \[ v_{0x} = 20.0\cos25^\circ \approx 18.13\,\text{m/s},\quad v_{0y} = 20.0\sin25^\circ \approx 8.45\,\text{m/s}. \]
    • Setting \(y(t)=0\) for the motion: \[ 10.0 + 8.45t - 4.9t^2 = 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad t \approx 2.53\,\text{s}. \]
  2. (b) Horizontal Range:
    • \(R = v_{0x}t \approx 18.13 \times 2.53 \approx 45.82\,\text{m}.\)
  3. (c) Height of \(15.0\,\text{m}\) Above Ground:
    • The maximum height reached is: \[ H_{\text{max}} = 10.0 + \frac{(8.45)^2}{2(9.8)} \approx 10.0 + 3.65 \approx 13.65\,\text{m}. \]
    • Since \(15.0\,\text{m} > 13.65\,\text{m}\), the projectile never reaches \(15.0\,\text{m}\) above the ground.

Problem 6: Simultaneous Launches with Different Angles

  1. (a) Which Object Hits the Ground First:
    • Object A (horizontal launch): From a \(15.0\,\text{m}\) ledge with \(v_{0y}=0\), \[ t_A = \sqrt{\frac{15.0}{4.9}} \approx 1.75\,\text{s}. \]
    • Object B (launched at \(45^\circ\)): With \(v_{0y} \approx 8.0\sin45^\circ \approx 5.66\,\text{m/s}\), solving \(15.0 + 5.66t - 4.9t^2 = 0\) gives \(t_B \approx 2.42\,\text{s}\).
    • Thus, Object A hits the ground first.
  2. (b) Time of Flight and Final Speed:
    • Object A:
      • Time: \(t_A \approx 1.75\,\text{s}\).
      • Final vertical speed: \(v_{Ay} \approx -9.8(1.75) \approx -17.15\,\text{m/s}\); horizontal remains \(8.0\,\text{m/s}\).
      • Final speed: \[ |\mathbf{v}_A| \approx \sqrt{8.0^2 + 17.15^2} \approx 18.92\,\text{m/s}. \]
    • Object B:
      • Time: \(t_B \approx 2.42\,\text{s}\).
      • Final vertical speed: \(v_{By} \approx 5.66 - 9.8(2.42) \approx -18.06\,\text{m/s}\); horizontal remains \(5.66\,\text{m/s}\).
      • Final speed: \[ |\mathbf{v}_B| \approx \sqrt{(5.66)^2 + (18.06)^2} \approx 18.92\,\text{m/s}. \]

Problem 7: 3D Projectile with Gravity in the \(z\)-Direction

  1. (a) Initial Speed and Angles:
    • Given components: \(v_x = 5.0\,\text{m/s}\), \(v_y = 10.0\,\text{m/s}\), \(v_z = 15.0\,\text{m/s}\).
    • Initial speed: \[ v_0 = \sqrt{5.0^2 + 10.0^2 + 15.0^2} \approx \sqrt{350} \approx 18.71\,\text{m/s}. \]
    • Angles with the axes: \[ \theta_x = \arccos\left(\frac{5.0}{18.71}\right) \approx 74.5^\circ,\quad \theta_y = \arccos\left(\frac{10.0}{18.71}\right) \approx 57.7^\circ,\quad \theta_z = \arccos\left(\frac{15.0}{18.71}\right) \approx 36.9^\circ. \]
  2. (b) Velocity Vector as a Function of Time:
    • Gravity only affects the \(z\)-component: \[ \mathbf{v}(t) = \bigl(5.0\,\hat{i} + 10.0\,\hat{j} + (15.0 - 9.8t)\,\hat{k}\bigr)\,\text{m/s}. \]
  3. (c) Velocity at \(t = 3.0\,\text{s}\):
    • \(v_x = 5.0\,\text{m/s}\), \(v_y = 10.0\,\text{m/s}\), and \[ v_z = 15.0 - 9.8(3.0) \approx 15.0 - 29.4 \approx -14.4\,\text{m/s}. \]

Problem 8: Integration-Based Motion in 2D

  1. (a) Vertical Position Function \(y(t)\):
    • For a stone thrown at \(22.0\,\text{m/s}\) at \(40^\circ\), the vertical component is: \[ v_{0y} = 22.0\sin40^\circ \approx 14.14\,\text{m/s}, \] so integrating the acceleration gives: \[ y(t) = 14.14t - 4.9t^2. \]
  2. (b) Displacements after \(2.0\,\text{s}\):
    • Horizontal displacement: \[ x = 22.0\cos40^\circ \times 2.0 \approx 16.85 \times 2.0 \approx 33.70\,\text{m}. \]
    • Vertical displacement: \[ y(2.0) \approx 14.14(2.0) - 4.9(4.0) \approx 28.28 - 19.6 \approx 8.68\,\text{m}. \]
  3. (c) Comparison with Maximum Height:
    • Maximum height is reached at: \[ t_{\text{max}} = \frac{14.14}{9.8} \approx 1.44\,\text{s}, \] with \[ y_{\text{max}} \approx 14.14(1.44) - 4.9(1.44)^2 \approx 10.21\,\text{m}. \]
    • At \(t = 2.0\,\text{s}\), \(y(2.0) \approx 8.68\,\text{m}\), which is lower than the maximum height.

Problem 9: Derivation of Range Expression via Integration

  1. (a) Derivation:
    • The horizontal position is given by: \[ x(t) = v\cos\theta\,t. \]
    • The time of flight on level ground is: \[ T = \frac{2v\sin\theta}{g}. \]
    • Therefore, the range is: \[ R = v\cos\theta\,T = \frac{2v^2\sin\theta\cos\theta}{g} = \frac{v^2\sin2\theta}{g}. \]
  2. (b) Range Calculation:
    • For \(v = 24.0\,\text{m/s}\) and \(\theta = 30^\circ\) (so \(\sin60^\circ \approx 0.866\)): \[ R = \frac{(24.0)^2\times0.866}{9.8} \approx \frac{576\times0.866}{9.8} \approx 51.0\,\text{m}. \]
  3. (c) Comparison with Standard Formula:
    • The derived expression, \(R = \frac{v^2\sin2\theta}{g}\), is identical to the standard formula.

Problem 10: 3D Position & Derivatives

  1. (a) Velocity:
    • Given \[ \mathbf{r}(t) = \bigl(2t\,\hat{i} + 3t^2\,\hat{j} + 4t^3\,\hat{k}\bigr), \] differentiating yields: \[ \mathbf{v}(t) = \bigl(2\,\hat{i} + 6t\,\hat{j} + 12t^2\,\hat{k}\bigr). \]
  2. (b) Acceleration:
    • Differentiating the velocity gives: \[ \mathbf{a}(t) = \bigl(0\,\hat{i} + 6\,\hat{j} + 24t\,\hat{k}\bigr). \]
  3. (c) Evaluation at \(t = 2.0\,\text{s}\):
    • Velocity: \[ \mathbf{v}(2.0) = \bigl(2,\, 6(2.0),\, 12(2.0)^2\bigr) = (2,\, 12,\, 48)\,\text{m/s}. \]
    • Acceleration: \[ \mathbf{a}(2.0) = \bigl(0,\, 6,\, 24(2.0)\bigr) = (0,\, 6,\, 48)\,\text{m/s}^2. \]
