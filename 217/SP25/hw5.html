Homework 5: Applications of Newton's Laws

Due: Tuesday, April 1, 2025, 11:59 PM

Problem 1 (Force & Friction): A 25.0 kg crate is initially at rest on a horizontal floor. The coefficient of static friction is 0.40 and the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.30.
(a) What minimum force is required to start the crate moving?
(b) Once the crate is moving, what force is needed to maintain a constant velocity?
(c) If a constant 130 N force is applied, find the crate's acceleration after it starts moving.

Problem 2 (Connected Blocks with Friction): Two blocks with masses m₁ = 5.0 kg and m₂ = 3.0 kg are connected by a light string that passes over a frictionless pulley. Block m₁ sits on a rough horizontal surface with coefficient of kinetic friction μₖ = 0.25, while m₂ hangs freely.
(a) Calculate the acceleration of the system.
(b) Determine the tension in the string.

Problem 3 (Inclined Plane & Friction): A 4.0 kg block slides down a 30° inclined plane. The coefficient of kinetic friction between the block and the plane is 0.20.
(a) Calculate the component of the weight parallel to the incline.
(b) Calculate the normal force and the frictional force.
(c) Find the net force and acceleration of the block.
(d) If the block starts from rest, how fast will it be moving after sliding 3.0 meters down the incline?

Problem 4 (Connected Blocks on Inclined Surfaces): Block A (6.0 kg) rests on a 25° incline. It is connected by a light cord over a frictionless pulley to Block B (4.0 kg) on a 40° incline. Both inclines are frictionless.
(a) Find the acceleration of the system and identify which way each block moves.
(b) Calculate the tension in the cord.
(c) If Block A starts 2.0 m up its incline from the pulley, how long will it take to reach the pulley?
Note: The given masses and incline angles yield nearly balanced gravitational components. Be sure to use enough significant figures to capture the small net acceleration.

Problem 5 (Stacked Blocks with External Force): A 5.0 kg block (Block A) sits on top of a 10.0 kg block (Block B), which rests on a horizontal, frictionless surface. The coefficient of static friction between the blocks is 0.35.
(a) What is the maximum force F that can be applied horizontally to Block B before Block A slips relative to Block B?
(b) If the applied force exceeds this value, find the acceleration of each block (assume that, upon slipping, the coefficient of kinetic friction between Block A and Block B is 0.30).
(c) What is the acceleration of Block B if F = 40.0 N and the blocks don't slip?

Problem 6 (Centripetal Force & Banked Curves): A 1200 kg car travels on a circular banked curve with radius 50.0 m. The curve is banked at an angle of 20°.
(a) If the road is frictionless, at what speed can the car take the curve without sliding up or down the banking?
(b) If the coefficient of static friction is 0.40, what is the maximum speed at which the car can take the curve?
(c) What is the minimum speed to avoid sliding down the banking?

Problem 7 (Uniform Circular Motion & Conical Pendulum): A 0.50 kg object is attached to a 1.2 m string and rotates in a horizontal circle. The string makes an angle of 30° with the vertical.
(a) Calculate the tension in the string.
(b) Find the speed of the object.
(c) Determine the period of rotation.

Problem 8 (Modified Atwood Machine with Incline): A 3.0 kg block (mass m₁) slides without friction on a 35° incline. It is connected by a light string that passes over a frictionless pulley to a hanging 2.5 kg block (mass m₂).
(a) Calculate the acceleration of the system.
(b) Find the tension in the string.
(c) If the blocks are released from rest and m₂ descends 0.80 m, what is its final velocity?

Problem 9 (Artificial Gravity in a Rotating Space Station): A space station is designed as a rotating cylinder with radius 85.0 m to create artificial gravity through centripetal acceleration. The angular velocity ω (in rad/s) is related to linear velocity v by v = ωr and to period T by ω = 2π/T.
(a) At what angular velocity must the station rotate to simulate Earth's gravity (9.8 m/s²) at the rim?
(b) What is the linear speed of a person standing at the rim?
(c) Calculate the period of rotation in seconds.
(d) If a 70.0 kg astronaut stands on a scale at the rim, what would the scale read?

Block m₁ is on a horizontal surface with a coefficient of kinetic friction μ₁ = 0.30, Block m₂ is on a frictionless 25° incline, and Block m₃ hangs vertically. Both pulleys are frictionless.
(a) Calculate the acceleration of the system.
(b) Find the tensions in both strings (T₁ between m₁ and m₂, and T₂ between m₂ and m₃).
(c) If the system is released from rest, how far does Block m₃ fall in 2.0 seconds?
