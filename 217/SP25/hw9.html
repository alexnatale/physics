Homework 9 – Statics, Gravitation & Oscillations

Due: Tuesday 10 June 2025, 11:59 PM

Unless stated otherwise, use g = 9.81 m s-2.

1. Supported beam
A 3.0 m, 35 kg beam rests on scales 0.30 m from each end with a 24 kg toolbox 0.60 m from the left end.
(a) Determine the forces (in newtons) indicated by the left and right scales.
(b) If the toolbox is slid to the midpoint, by what percentage does each reading change?

2. Ladder in equilibrium
A 5.0 m, 18 kg ladder leans 65° against a frictionless wall. A 75 kg painter stands 3.5 m up. The ladder’s center of gravity is midway along its length.
(a) Calculate the static-friction force at the floor.
(b) Determine the minimum coefficient of static friction μs needed to prevent slipping.

3. Axial load on a steel rod
A 2.0 m steel rod (Young’s modulus E = 2.0 × 1011 Pa, cross-sectional area A = 1.5 cm2) supports a tensile load F = 1.2 × 104 N.
Use the relations stress σ = F/A, strain ε = σ/E, and elongation ΔL = εL.
(a) Compute σ, ε, and ΔL.
(b) The elastic‐limit stress is 250 MPa. State whether the rod stays within the elastic range by comparing σ with this limit (quantitative yes/no).

4. Earth–Moon gravitational interaction
Use Newton’s law \(F = G\frac{M_E M_M}{r^2}\) with
G = 6.674 × 10-11 N m2 kg-2, r = 3.84 × 108 m,
ME = 5.97 × 1024 kg, MM = 7.35 × 1022 kg.
(a) Calculate the magnitude of the Earth–Moon gravitational force.
(b) Determine the Moon’s acceleration toward Earth using a = F/MM.
(c) Using a centripetal-motion relation \(a = v^2/r\), compute the Moon’s orbital speed v and then its sidereal period \(T = 2\pi r/v\).

5. Incoming asteroid
An asteroid (mass m = 6.0 × 109 kg) approaches Earth from effectively “infinite” distance with speed v∞ = 2.0 km s-1.
Conservation of mechanical energy gives \(\tfrac12 m v_{\infty}^2 = \tfrac12 m v^2 - \dfrac{G M_E m}{R_E + h}\).
Take ME = 5.97 × 1024 kg, RE = 6.37 × 106 m, h = 100 km.
(a) Find the asteroid’s speed v 100 km above Earth’s surface.
(b) Earth’s escape speed at that altitude is \(v_{\text{esc}} = \sqrt{\tfrac{2GM_E}{R_E + h}}\). Compute vesc and state quantitatively whether the asteroid remains gravitationally bound (compare v with vesc).

6. 300 km circular orbit
A spacecraft orbits 300 km above Earth’s surface.
The orbital-speed formula is \(v = \sqrt{\dfrac{GM_E}{R_E + h}}\), and the orbital period is \(T = \dfrac{2\pi (R_E + h)}{v}\).
(a) Calculate v.
(b) Determine T.
Use ME = 5.97 × 1024 kg, RE = 6.37 × 106 m.

7. Exoplanet via Kepler’s third law
A Jupiter-size planet orbits a 1.40 M☉ star every P = 380 days (3.28 × 107 s).
Kepler’s third-law form: \(a^3 = \dfrac{G M_{\star} P^2}{4\pi^2}\).
(a) Compute the semimajor axis a (in m and AU).
(b) Treating the orbit as circular, find the orbital speed v = 2πa/P.
(c) Determine the star’s gravitational acceleration on the planet at that distance, ag = GM★/a2.

8. Mass–spring simple harmonic motion
A 0.60 kg block oscillates on a horizontal spring with force constant k = 45 N m-1 and amplitude A = 0.12 m.
For SHM, \(x(t)=A\cos(\omega t+\phi)\) with angular frequency \(\omega=\sqrt{k/m}\).
(a) Write x(t) assuming the block starts at +A and is momentarily at rest.
(b) Determine the period T = 2π/ω and maximum speed vmax = Aω.
(c) If the amplitude doubles, by what factor does the total mechanical energy \(E=\tfrac12 kA^2\) change?

9. Physical pendulum
A 1.2 m, 2.5 kg uniform rod pivots about one end. The moment of inertia about that pivot is \(I = \tfrac{1}{3}mL^2\).
For small angles, the period is \(T = 2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{I}{mg(\tfrac12 L)}}\). Calculate T.

10. Kepler-law comparison for two Martian satellites
Two reconnaissance satellites, A and B, follow **elliptical** orbits around Mars (mass MM = 6.42 × 1023 kg).
Satellite A has semimajor axis aA = 5.40 × 106 m and eccentricity eA = 0.20.
Kepler’s period law (in SI units): \(T = 2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{a^3}{GM}}\).
Vis-viva equation: \(v = \sqrt{\,GM\!\left(\dfrac{2}{r} - \dfrac{1}{a}\right)}\).
(a) Calculate A’s orbital period TA.
(b) Satellite B shares the **same periapsis radius** as A. Its orbital period is exactly one-quarter that of A (TB = TA/4). Using the ratio form \(T_1^2/T_2^2 = a_1^3/a_2^3\), determine B’s semimajor axis aB.
(c) If B’s eccentricity is eB = 0.10, compute its apoapsis radius rap,B = aB(1+eB).
(d) Find the periapsis speed of each satellite using the vis-viva equation (set r = a(1-e) for each) and state which is faster and by what percentage.
