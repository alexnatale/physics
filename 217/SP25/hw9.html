Phys 217 Homework 9

Homework 5 – Statics, Gravitation & Oscillations

Due: Tuesday 10 June 2025, 11:59 PM

Unless stated otherwise, use g = 9.81 m s-2.

1. Supported beam
A 3.0 m, 35 kg beam rests on scales 0.30 m from each end with a 24 kg toolbox 0.60 m from the left end.
(a) Determine the forces (in newtons) indicated by the left and right scales.
(b) If the toolbox is slid to the midpoint, by what percentage does each reading change?

2. Ladder in equilibrium
A 5.0 m, 18 kg ladder leans 65° against a frictionless wall. A 75 kg painter stands 3.5 m up.
(a) Calculate the static-friction force at the floor.
(b) Determine the minimum coefficient of static friction μs needed to prevent slipping.

3. Axial load on a steel rod
A 2.0 m steel rod (E = 2.0 × 1011 Pa, area = 1.5 cm2) supports 1.2 × 104 N.
(a) Compute the stress, strain, and elongation ΔL.
(b) Check whether the rod exceeds the 250 MPa elastic limit.

4. Earth–Moon gravitational pull
Calculate the magnitude of the Earth–Moon force using Newton’s law of gravitation (r = 3.84 × 108 m). Use ME = 5.97 × 1024 kg and MM = 7.35 × 1022 kg.
Comment on one everyday phenomenon this interaction produces.

5. Incoming asteroid
An asteroid (6.0 × 109 kg) approaches Earth from “infinity” at 2.0 km s-1.
(a) Use energy conservation to find its speed 100 km above Earth’s surface.
(b) Compare that speed with Earth’s escape speed there and state whether the asteroid will remain bound.
Assume Earth’s mass ME = 5.97 × 1024 kg and radius RE = 6.37 × 106 m.

6. 300 km circular orbit
A spacecraft orbits 300 km above Earth.
(a) Calculate its orbital speed.
(b) Determine the orbital period.
Use ME = 5.97 × 1024 kg and RE = 6.37 × 106 m.

7. Exoplanet via Kepler
A Jupiter-size planet orbits a 1.4 M☉ star every 380 days.
(a) Use Kepler’s third law to find the semimajor axis.
(b) Calculate the planet’s orbital speed at that distance.

8. Mass–spring SHM
A 0.60 kg block on a 45 N m-1 spring oscillates with amplitude 0.12 m.
(a) Write x(t) if it starts at +0.12 m, momentarily at rest.
(b) Determine the period and maximum speed.
(c) If the amplitude doubles, by what factor does the total mechanical energy change?

9. Physical pendulum
A 1.2 m, 2.5 kg uniform rod pivots about one end. Calculate its small-angle period.

10. Resonance & damping (conceptual)
The amplitude of a driven, damped oscillator is
\(A(\omega_d)=\dfrac{F_0}{\sqrt{(k-m\omega_d^2)^2+(b\omega_d)^2}}.\)
(a) In words, describe how increasing the damping coefficient b changes the resonance peak.
(b) Give one real-world system where added damping prevents damage.
