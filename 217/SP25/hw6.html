Homework 6: Work and Energy

Due: Tuesday, April 29, 2025, 11:59 PM

Problem 1 (Basic Work Calculation): A delivery person pushes a 35.0 kg package across a horizontal floor with a force of 75.0 N at an angle of 25° below the horizontal.
(a) Calculate the work done by the applied force if the package is pushed 12.0 meters.
(b) If the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.15, calculate the work done by friction.
(c) What is the net work done on the package?

Problem 2 (Work-Energy Theorem): A 1500 kg car accelerates from rest to 25.0 m/s.
(a) What is the change in kinetic energy of the car?
(b) How much work was done to achieve this change in energy?
(c) If this acceleration happened over a distance of 120 meters, what was the average net force applied to the car?

Problem 3 (Power Calculations): A 65.0 kg person runs up a staircase to a height of 4.5 meters in 6.0 seconds.
(a) Calculate the work done against gravity.
(b) Calculate the power output in watts.
(c) Convert this power to horsepower (1 hp = 746 W).
(d) If the person's actual power output is 380 W, calculate the efficiency of this process.

Problem 4 (Gravitational Potential Energy): A 2.0 kg rock is thrown vertically upward with an initial speed of 15.0 m/s from a height of 1.2 meters above the ground.
(a) Calculate the initial kinetic energy of the rock.
(b) Calculate the initial gravitational potential energy of the rock relative to the ground.
(c) What is the maximum height reached by the rock relative to the ground?
(d) What is the speed of the rock when it hits the ground?

Problem 5 (Elastic Potential Energy): A spring with spring constant k = 200 N/m is initially compressed 15.0 cm from its equilibrium position.
(a) Calculate the elastic potential energy stored in the spring.
(b) If the spring is used to launch a 0.50 kg ball, what is the maximum speed the ball can achieve?
(c) If instead the spring is released with the 0.50 kg ball attached and moving vertically, how high above the release point can the ball go?

Problem 6 (Energy with Non-Conservative Forces): A 5.0 kg block slides down a 3.0 meter long incline that makes an angle of 30° with the horizontal. The coefficient of kinetic friction between the block and the incline is 0.25.
(a) Calculate the work done by gravity on the block.
(b) Calculate the work done by friction on the block.
(c) Calculate the final speed of the block if it starts from rest at the top of the incline.
(d) How much thermal energy is generated in this process?

Problem 7 (Conservation of Mechanical Energy): A 1.0 kg pendulum bob is released from rest when the pendulum makes a 30° angle with the vertical. The pendulum length is 2.0 meters.
(a) What is the initial gravitational potential energy relative to the lowest point of the pendulum's path?
(b) What is the speed of the pendulum bob at the lowest point of its swing?
(c) To what angle on the other side will the pendulum swing? (Assume no energy losses)
(d) If 8% of the mechanical energy is lost due to air resistance during a complete back-and-forth swing, what would be the angle on the return swing?

Problem 8 (Energy Conversion): A roller coaster car with mass 320 kg (including passengers) starts from rest at the top of a hill 45.0 meters above the ground.
(a) What is the gravitational potential energy of the car at the top of the hill relative to the ground?
(b) The car descends and then climbs a second hill. If the car's speed at the top of the second hill is 8.0 m/s, and this hill is 25.0 meters high, how much mechanical energy was converted to thermal energy during the ride?
(c) What percentage of the initial mechanical energy was lost?

Problem 9 (Potential Energy and Stability): A particle of mass 0.25 kg moves along the x-axis in a potential given by U(x) = 2x² - 12x + 18 joules, where x is in meters.
(a) Find the positions of all equilibrium points.
(b) Determine which equilibrium points are stable and which are unstable.
(c) If the particle has a total energy of 0 joules, determine the range of positions where the particle can be found.
(d) Calculate the maximum speed of the particle if its total energy is 0 joules.

Problem 10 (Energy Conversion Efficiency): A solar panel system has an area of 8.0 m² and receives solar radiation with an intensity of 1000 W/m².
(a) How much solar energy strikes the panel each second?
(b) If the solar cells convert 18% of the incident energy to electrical energy, what is the power output in watts?
(c) If this system is used to lift a 50.0 kg mass, how high can it raise the mass in 10.0 seconds? (Assume all electrical energy is used for lifting)
(d) If the solar panel powers a motor with 75% efficiency that drives a pump with 60% efficiency, what is the overall efficiency of converting solar energy to mechanical energy?
