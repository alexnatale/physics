Homework 3: Projectile Motion & Motion in 2 and 3 Dimensions

Due: Tuesday, March 18, 2025, 11:59 PM

Problem 1 (Classic Projectile Variation):
A ball is launched from ground level with an initial speed of \(18.0\,\text{m/s}\) at an angle of \(35^\circ\) above the horizontal.
(a) Calculate its time of flight, maximum height, and horizontal range.
(b) Explain why, if air resistance is neglected, the horizontal velocity remains constant.

Problem 2 (Horizontal Launch Variation):
A marble is fired horizontally from the edge of a \(20.0\,\text{m}\) high platform with an initial speed of \(6.0\,\text{m/s}\).
(a) Determine the time until impact using \(a_y = -9.8\,\text{m/s}^2\).
(b) Compute the horizontal distance traveled before hitting the ground.
(c) Find the magnitude and direction of the velocity just before impact.

Problem 3 (Initial Velocity Components):
A cannon fires a projectile with an initial speed of \(30.0\,\text{m/s}\) at an angle of \(50^\circ\) above the horizontal.
(a) Find the horizontal and vertical components of the initial velocity.
(b) Sketch a free-body diagram showing the forces acting on the projectile (ignore air resistance).
(c) Calculate the time for the projectile to return to the ground.

Problem 4 (Velocity at a Specific Time):
An object is launched with an initial speed of \(12.0\,\text{m/s}\) at an angle of \(70^\circ\) above the horizontal.
(a) Write the parametric equations \(x(t)\) and \(y(t)\) for its motion.
(b) Determine the velocity vector at \(t = 1.5\,\text{s}\).
(c) Compare the speed at \(t = 1.5\,\text{s}\) with the initial speed.

Problem 5 (Launch from an Elevated Platform):
A projectile is launched from a platform \(10.0\,\text{m}\) above the ground with an initial speed of \(20.0\,\text{m/s}\) at an angle of \(25^\circ\) above the horizontal.
(a) Find the total time the projectile is in the air.
(b) Determine the horizontal range.
(c) At what horizontal distance does the projectile reach a height of \(15.0\,\text{m}\) above the ground?

Problem 6 (Simultaneous Launches with Different Angles):
Two objects are thrown simultaneously from a \(15.0\,\text{m}\) high ledge:
• Object A: Thrown horizontally with a speed of \(8.0\,\text{m/s}\).
• Object B: Thrown at an angle of \(45^\circ\) above the horizontal with a speed of \(8.0\,\text{m/s}\).
(a) Which object hits the ground first?
(b) Calculate the time of flight and final speed for each object.

Problem 7 (3D Projectile with Gravity in the \(z\)-Direction):
A projectile is launched in 3D space with initial velocity components \(v_x = 5.0\,\text{m/s}\), \(v_y = 10.0\,\text{m/s}\), and \(v_z = 15.0\,\text{m/s}\).
Assume gravity acts in the negative \(z\)-direction with \(g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2\).
(a) Compute the initial speed and the angles between the velocity vector and the \(x\), \(y\), and \(z\) axes.
(b) Write the velocity vector as a function of time.
(c) Evaluate the velocity components at \(t = 3.0\,\text{s}\).

Problem 8 (Integration-Based Motion in 2D):
A stone is thrown upward from ground level at an angle of \(40^\circ\) with an initial speed of \(22.0\,\text{m/s}\).
Given the vertical acceleration \(a_y = -9.8\,\text{m/s}^2\):
(a) Set up and integrate to find the vertical position function \(y(t)\).
(b) Calculate the horizontal and vertical displacements after \(2.0\,\text{s}\).
(c) Determine how the height at \(2.0\,\text{s}\) compares to the maximum height.

Problem 9 (Derivation of Range Expression via Integration):
(a) Derive an expression for the horizontal range \(R\) of a projectile launched with speed \(v\) at an angle \(\theta\) on level ground using integration.
(b) Calculate the range for \(v = 24.0\,\text{m/s}\) and \(\theta = 30^\circ\).
(c) Compare your result to the standard formula \(R = \frac{v^2}{g}\sin(2\theta)\).

Problem 10 (3D Position & Derivatives):
A particle’s position is given by \( \mathbf{r}(t) = \bigl(2t\,\hat{\mathbf{i}} + 3t^2\,\hat{\mathbf{j}} + 4t^3\,\hat{\mathbf{k}} \bigr)\,\text{meters} \).
(a) Differentiate to find its velocity at any time \(t\).
(b) Differentiate again to find its acceleration.
(c) Evaluate both velocity and acceleration at \(t = 2.0\,\text{s}\).
