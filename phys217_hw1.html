Interactive Physics Problems

Problem 1: Starting with the definition 1.00 in. = 2.54 cm, find the number of (a) kilometers in 6.739 miles and (b) number of feet in 1.00 km.

Problem 2: A plot of land has an area of 16.7 acres. If 1 acre is 4047 m², and a hectare has the same area as a plot of 100.0 m by 100.0 m, how many hectares are in this plot?

Problem 3: Determine the percent error in the approximate value of π×10⁷ s for the number of seconds in a year, given that there are 365.24 days in a year.

Problem 4: Four astronauts are in a spherical space station. If each of them typically breathes about 552 cm³ of air with each breath, and take 18 breaths per minute, what is the volume of air you would need in the space station if these four astronauts stayed for a full year? If the density of air is 1.25 kg/m³ and it costs $86.7/kg to send objects to the space station, how much money would the astronaut air supply cost?

Problem 5: If 1 au is 1.49 × 10¹¹ m, and a light-year is the distance light travels in 1 year (light travels at c = 3.0 × 10⁸ m/s), then how many au is it to get to Alpha Centauri if it is 4.367 light years away?

Problem 6: (a) The recommended daily allowance (RDA) of the trace metal magnesium is 33 mg/day for newborns. Express this quantity in μg/day. (b) For adults, the RDA of the amino acid lysine is 12 mg per kg of body mass. How many grams per day should a 60 kg adult receive? (c) A typical multivitamin tablet can contain 2.1 mg of vitamin B₂ (riboflavin), and the RDA is 0.0030 g/day. How many such tablets should a person take each day to get the proper amount of this vitamin, if they get none from other sources? (d) The RDA for the trace element of selenium is 6.6e-5 g/day. Express that dose in mg/day.

Problem 7: A particular type of hypothetical spacecraft rotates around a central point to create artificial gravity, and is like a hollow, long, cylinder. To be comfortable, people need at least 20 m³ of space per person. If the outer diameter of this space craft is 16.1 m, the inner diameter is 10.1 m, and the cylindrical spin section is 11 m long, how many people (round to nearest whole person) can comfortably fit in the spacecraft?
