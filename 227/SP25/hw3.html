Homework 3: Gauss’s Law and Electric Dipoles

Due: Tuesday, Mar 18, 2025, 11:59 PM
Topics: Applications of Gauss’s Law and the behavior of Electric Dipoles (external fields, torque, potential energy)
Problems: 10 multi‐part problems — 5 on Gauss’s Law/Electric Fields and 5 on Electric Dipoles.

Problem 1 (Spherical Gaussian Surface):
(a) Use Gauss’s Law to derive the expression for the electric flux through a spherical surface enclosing a point charge \(q=+1.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) with radius \(R=0.20\,m\).
(b) Compute the net flux.

Problem 2 (Infinite Plane of Charge):
(a) Use Gauss’s Law to derive the electric field \(E=\frac{\sigma}{2\varepsilon_0}\) for an infinite plane with surface charge density \(\sigma\).
(b) Calculate \(E\) for \(\sigma=5.0\times10^{-6}\,C/m^2\).

Problem 3 (Uniformly Charged Cylinder):
(a) Apply Gauss’s Law to derive expressions for the electric flux and field inside (\(r < R\)) and outside (\(r \ge R\)) an infinitely long cylinder of radius \(R = 0.10\,m\) carrying a uniform volume charge density \(\rho = 2.0\times10^{4}\,C/m^3\).
(b) Compute the field at \(r = 0.05\,m\) and \(r = 0.15\,m\).

Problem 4 (Uniformly Charged Sphere):
(a) Derive the expressions for the electric field inside \(r < R\) and outside \(r \ge R\) a sphere with uniform charge density \(\rho\) using Gauss’s Law.
(b) For a sphere with \(R = 0.15\,m\) and \(\rho = 1.0 \times 10^{4}\,C/m^3\), compute the field at \(r = 0.10\,m\) and \(r = 0.30\,m\).

Problem 5 (Spherical Shell):
(a) For a spherical shell of radius \(R=0.25\,m\) with a uniformly distributed total charge \(Q=+2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\), use Gauss’s Law to determine the net electric flux.
(b) Evaluate the flux numerically.

Problem 6 (Torque on an Electric Dipole):
An electric dipole consists of charges \(+q\) and \(-q\) separated by a distance \(d\) (with dipole moment \(\mathbf{p}=q\,d\)). It is placed in a uniform external electric field \(\mathbf{E}_0\).
For \(q=2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\), \(d=0.04\,m\), and \(\mathbf{E}_0=5.0\times10^3\,N/C\) (directed along the x-axis), calculate the magnitude of the torque when the dipole makes an angle of \(30^\circ\) with the field.

Problem 7 (Potential Energy of an Electric Dipole):
Calculate \(U\) for a dipole with \(q=2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) and \(d=0.04\,m\) when \(\theta=45^\circ\) and \(\mathbf{E}_0=5.0\times10^3\,N/C\).

Problem 8 (Electric Field on the Perpendicular Bisector of a Dipole):
The magnitude of the electric field on the perpendicular bisector of an ideal dipole is given by \( E_{\text{perp}} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{p}{r^3} \), where \( p = q\,d \) is the dipole moment. Calculate the magnitude of the electric field at a distance \( r = 0.25\,m \) from the center of a dipole with \( q = 1.0\times10^{-7}\,C \) and \( d = 0.05\,m \).

Problem 9 (Work Done to Rotate an Electric Dipole):
The work done to rotate an electric dipole in a uniform external electric field from an initial angle of \(0^\circ\) to an angle \(\theta\) is given by \( W = p\,E_0\,(1-\cos\theta) \). Calculate the work required to rotate a dipole with \( q = 1.0\times10^{-7}\,C \) and \( d = 0.05\,m \) (so that \( p = q\,d \)) in a field of \( E_0 = 5.0\times10^3\,N/C \) from \(0^\circ\) to \(60^\circ\).

Problem 10 (Superposition of Electric Fields):
A point charge \(q=+1.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) is located at \((0,0)\) and an electric dipole (with \(q=1.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) and \(d=0.05\,m\)) is centered at \((0.20\,m, 0)\).
(a) Compute the net electric field at the point \((0.10\,m, 0)\) by vector addition of the field from the point charge and the dipole.
(b) Describe the direction of the resulting field.
