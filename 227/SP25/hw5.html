Topics: Capacitance, Dielectrics, and Energy Storage in Capacitors.
Note: All key equations have been derived and explained in class. Use these equations to complete the problems.

Problem 1 (Parallel-Plate Capacitor):
Using the formula \( C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{A}{d} \), calculate the capacitance for a parallel-plate capacitor with \( A = 0.02\,m^2 \) and \( d = 0.001\,m \). Then, determine the new capacitance when a dielectric with \( k = 4.5 \) fills the gap.

Problem 2 (Energy Storage in a Capacitor):
A capacitor from Problem 1 is charged to \( V = 100\,V \). Using \( U = \frac{1}{2} C V^2 \), calculate the energy stored before and after the dielectric is introduced (the 100 voltage charges the air capacitor and is kept connected as the dielectric is inserted). Then repeat it assuming constant charge (the 100 voltage charges the air capacitor, then that is removed and the dielectric is inserted).

Problem 3 (Partial Dielectric Insertion):
A parallel-plate capacitor with an initial capacitance of \( 2.00\,\mu F \) is operating at \( 50\,V \). A dielectric with \( k = 3.0 \) is inserted only halfway between the plates. Calculate the effective capacitance and compare the energy stored before and after the dielectric insertion (assume constant voltage).

Problem 4 (Spherical Capacitor):
Using the formula \( C = 4\pi\varepsilon_0\frac{R_1 R_2}{R_2 - R_1} \), calculate the capacitance for a spherical capacitor with \( R_1 = 0.05\,m \) and \( R_2 = 0.10\,m \).

Problem 5 (Cylindrical Capacitor):
For a cylindrical capacitor with inner radius \( a = 0.01\,m \), outer radius \( b = 0.02\,m \), and length \( L = 1.0\,m \), calculate its capacitance per unit length using \( C/L = \frac{2\pi\varepsilon_0}{\ln(b/a)} \).

Problem 6 (Capacitor Bank Design):
(a) Determine the total capacitance required to store \( 0.50\,J \) of energy at \( 100\,V \).
(b) Propose a configuration using identical \( 5.00\,\mu F \) capacitors (state whether they are arranged in series, parallel, or a combination) to achieve this capacitance.

Problem 7 (Composite Dielectric Capacitor):
A parallel-plate capacitor has a plate area of \( A = 0.015\,m^2 \) and a separation \( d = 0.002\,m \). The space between the plates is filled in two equal halves: the bottom half with a dielectric of constant \( k_1 = 2.5 \) and the top half with a dielectric of constant \( k_2 = 4.0 \).
Calculate the effective capacitance of this composite capacitor.

Problem 8 (Capacitor Bank Design):
(a) Determine the total capacitance required to store \( 0.50\,J \) of energy at an operating voltage of \( 100\,V \).
(b) Propose a configuration using identical \( 5.00\,\mu F \) capacitors (state whether they are arranged in series, parallel, or a combination) to achieve the target capacitance.

Problem 9 (Capacitor Bank Design):
(a) Determine the total capacitance required to store \(0.50\,J\) of energy at \(V=100\,V\).
(b) Propose a configuration using identical \(5.00\,\mu F\) capacitors to achieve this capacitance.

Problem 10 (Dielectric Breakdown and Safe Operating Voltage):
A parallel-plate capacitor is constructed using a dielectric material that has a dielectric strength of \(3.0\times10^6\,\text{V/m}\). The capacitor has a plate area of \(A = 0.02\,m^2\) and a plate separation of \(d = 0.001\,m\).
(a) Calculate the maximum voltage that can be safely applied before the dielectric breaks down.
(b) Using the formula \( C=\varepsilon_0 \frac{A}{d} \), determine the capacitance of the capacitor in vacuum, and then find the maximum charge it can store under these safe operating conditions.
(c) Briefly explain how using a dielectric with a higher dielectric constant might affect both the capacitance and the breakdown voltage.
