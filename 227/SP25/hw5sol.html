Solutions Guide – Capacitor Problem Set

Question 1 – Parallel-Plate Capacitor

\(C=\varepsilon_{0}\dfrac{A}{d}\)

\(C = (8.854\times10^{-12}\,\mathrm{F\,m^{-1}})\, \dfrac{0.0200\;\mathrm{m^{2}}}{0.00100\;\mathrm{m}} = 1.77\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm{F}=177\;\mathrm{pF}\).

With a dielectric of k = 4.5: \(C' = kC = 797\;\mathrm{pF}\).

Question 2 – Energy Storage in a Capacitor

Using \(U=\tfrac12CV^{2}\) with V = 100 V.

  • Battery connected (constant V): \(U_{\text{air}} = 0.885\;\mu\text{J}\), \(U_{\text{diel}} = 3.98\;\mu\text{J}\).
  • Battery disconnected (constant Q): new voltage \(22.2\;\text{V}\), energy \(U' = 0.198\;\mu\text{J}\).

Question 3 – Partial Dielectric Insertion

Effective capacitance \(C_{\text{eff}} = C_{0}\dfrac{1+k}{2}=4.00\;\mu\text{F}\).

Energy doubles from 2.50 mJ to 5.00 mJ at 50 V.

Question 4 – Spherical Capacitor

\(C = 4\pi\varepsilon_{0}\dfrac{R_{1}R_{2}}{R_{2}-R_{1}} = 11.1\;\text{pF}\)

Question 5 – Cylindrical Capacitor per Unit Length

\( \dfrac{C}{L} = \dfrac{2\pi\varepsilon_{0}}{\ln(b/a)} = 80.3\;\text{pF m}^{-1}\)

Question 6 – Capacitor Bank Design (I)

Required \(C = \dfrac{2U}{V^{2}} = 100\;\mu\text{F}\). Use 20 identical 5 µF capacitors in parallel.

Question 7 – Composite Dielectric Capacitor

Two half-thickness layers in series → \(C_{\text{eff}} = 204\;\text{pF}\).

Questions 8 & 9 – Capacitor Bank Design (duplicates)

Same target as Q6 → 100 µF total via 20 × 5 µF in parallel.

Question 10 – Dielectric Breakdown & Safe Limits

  1. Safe voltage \(V_{\max}=E_{\max}d = 3.00\;\text{kV}\).
  2. Capacitance \(C=177\;\text{pF}\); charge limit \(Q_{\max}=CV_{\max}=0.531\;\mu\text{C}\).
  3. A higher-k dielectric raises \(C\) but safe voltage depends on the material’s own breakdown strength; designers balance both properties.
