Homework 1: Vectors, Electric Charge, and Coulomb's Law

Due: Wednesday, Feb 19, 2025, 11:59 PM
Topics: Vector operations (including dot and cross products) and problems involving electric charge and Coulomb's law (no electric field calculations).
Problems: 10 multi‐part problems — 5 on vector operations and 5 on electric charge & Coulomb's law.

Problem 1 (Advanced Vector Operations):
(a) Given \(\mathbf{A} = (3\,\hat{i} - 4\,\hat{j} + 2\,\hat{k})\), compute its magnitude and write its unit vector.
(b) For \(\mathbf{B} = (1\,\hat{i} + 2\,\hat{j} - 2\,\hat{k})\), calculate \(\mathbf{A}\cdot\mathbf{B}\) and discuss the significance of its sign.
(c) Compute \(\mathbf{A}\times\mathbf{B}\) and verify that it is perpendicular to both \(\mathbf{A}\) and \(\mathbf{B}\).

Problem 2 (Vector Addition & Decomposition):
(a) Given \(\mathbf{C} = (2\,\hat{i} + 3\,\hat{j})\) and \(\mathbf{D} = (-1\,\hat{i} + 4\,\hat{j})\), compute the resultant vector \(\mathbf{R} = \mathbf{C} + \mathbf{D}\).
(b) Express \(\mathbf{R}\) in polar form (magnitude and direction from the +x-axis).
(c) Verify the computed magnitude from the components.

Problem 3 (Projection and Angle Between Vectors):
(a) Find the projection (dot product) of \(\mathbf{A} = (4\,\hat{i} - 2\,\hat{j} + \hat{k})\) onto \(\mathbf{B} = (1\,\hat{i} + \hat{j} + 0\,\hat{k})\).
(b) Calculate the angle between \(\mathbf{A}\) and \(\mathbf{B}\) using the dot product.

Problem 4 (Resultant of Multiple Vectors):
Given \(\mathbf{P} = (2\,\hat{i} + 0\,\hat{j} + 3\,\hat{k})\), \(\mathbf{Q} = (-1\,\hat{i} + 4\,\hat{j} + 0\,\hat{k})\), and \(\mathbf{R} = (0\,\hat{i} - 2\,\hat{j} + 5\,\hat{k})\), compute the resultant vector \(\mathbf{S} = \mathbf{P} + \mathbf{Q} + \mathbf{R}\) and its magnitude.

Problem 5 (Cross Product and Area Calculation):
(a) Given \(\mathbf{U} = (1\,\hat{i} + 3\,\hat{j} - 2\,\hat{k})\) and \(\mathbf{V} = (4\,\hat{i} - 1\,\hat{j} + 2\,\hat{k})\), compute \(\mathbf{U}\times\mathbf{V}\).
(b) Determine the area of the parallelogram defined by \(\mathbf{U}\) and \(\mathbf{V}\).

Problem 6 (Coulomb Force Between Two Charges):
Two point charges \(q_1 = +2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) and \(q_2 = -3.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) are separated by \(0.15\,m\).
(a) Use Coulomb's law to calculate the magnitude of the force between them.
(b) Indicate the direction of the force on each charge.

Problem 7 (Superposition of Coulomb Forces):
Three point charges are aligned along the x-axis: \(q_1 = +3.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) at \(x=0\), \(q_2 = -1.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) at \(x=0.20\,m\), and \(q_3 = +2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) at \(x=0.35\,m\).
Compute the net Coulomb force on \(q_2\) due to \(q_1\) and \(q_3\), and discuss how the signs of the charges affect the direction of the forces.

Problem 8 (Effect of Separation on Coulomb Force):
Two point charges, \(q_1 = +2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) and \(q_2 = -2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\), are initially separated by \(0.10\,m\).
(a) Calculate the magnitude of the Coulomb force between the charges using Coulomb's law.
(b) If the separation between the charges is increased by 20%, determine the new force and compare it to the original force.
(c) Explain how the inverse square dependence of Coulomb's law affects the force when the separation changes.

Problem 9 (Equilibrium Position for a Test Charge):
Two fixed point charges, \(q_1 = +3.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) and \(q_2 = +1.0\times10^{-7}\,C\), are separated by \(0.50\,m\).
Determine the point along the line joining them where a test charge \(q_0 = -1.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) would experience zero net force.
Provide a qualitative explanation for your result.

Problem 10 (Coulomb Force in a Triangular Charge Configuration):
Three point charges are placed at the vertices of an equilateral triangle with side length 0.20 m:
qA = +2.0 × 10-7 C, qB = -2.0 × 10-7 C, qC = -2.0 × 10-7 C.

(a) Calculate the net Coulomb force acting on qA.
(b) Describe the direction of the net force relative to the geometry of the triangle.
