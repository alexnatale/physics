Problem 1 (Lorentz‑Force Concepts & Calculation):
(a) Qualitatively explain how the direction of the magnetic force on a charge depends on the sign of the charge (use the right‑hand rule).
(b) For \(q = 1.00\times10^{-7}\,\text{C}\), \(\mathbf v = 5.00\times10^{3}\,\text{m/s}\,\hat{\mathbf x}\), \(\mathbf B = 0.50\,\text{T}\,\hat{\mathbf z}\), and \(\mathbf E = 0\), calculate the magnetic‑force vector \(\mathbf F\).
(c) How would the magnitude of the force change if \(\mathbf v\) were parallel to \(\mathbf B\)? Explain briefly.
(d) If the charge were doubled while all other quantities stayed the same, by what factor would \(|\mathbf F|\) change?

Problem 2 (Uniform Circular Motion of a Charged Particle):
A particle of mass \(m\) and charge \(|q|\) moves perpendicular to a uniform field so its radius is \(r = \dfrac{mv}{|q|B}\).
(a) Using \(m = 2.00\times10^{-3}\,\text{kg}\), \(v = 3.00\times10^{3}\,\text{m/s}\), \(|q| = 1.00\times10^{-7}\,\text{C}\), \(B = 0.75\,\text{T}\), compute \(r\).
(b) Determine the cyclotron (angular) frequency and period of the motion.
(c) Conceptual: If the mass were halved while \(v,\;|q|,\;B\) stay the same, what happens to \(r\) and to the frequency?

Problem 3 (Field Around a Long Straight Wire):
The field magnitude at distance \(r\) from a long straight wire is \(B(r)=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}\).
(a) Calculate \(B\) at \(r = 0.10\,\text{m}\) for \(I = 5.00\,\text{A}\).
(b) Describe the orientation of the magnetic‑field lines around the wire and how the right‑hand rule determines their direction.
(c) If the current is doubled, by what factor does \(B\) at the same \(r\) change?

Problem 4 (Field at the Center of a Circular Loop):
For a single loop, the field at the center is \(B = \dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2r}\).
(a) Evaluate \(B\) for \(r = 0.15\,\text{m}\), \(I = 3.00\,\text{A}\).
(b) If the loop is replaced by \(N = 25\) tightly wound turns (same radius), compute the new field.
(c) Conceptual: Explain why increasing \(N\) increases the field linearly rather than quadratically.

Problem 5 (Field Inside a Long Solenoid):
Inside an ideal long solenoid, \(B=\mu_0 n I\).
(a) For \(n = 500\,\text{turns/m}\) and \(I = 2.00\,\text{A}\), compute \(B\).
(b) Determine the magnetic‑energy density \(u = \tfrac{B^{2}}{2\mu_0}\) inside the solenoid.
(c) Conceptual: Why does the field near the solenoid’s ends deviate from the ideal value?

Problem 6 (Field Inside a Toroid):
Within a toroid, \(B(r)=\dfrac{\mu_0 N I}{2\pi r}\) for \(a \le r \le b\).
(a) Evaluate \(B\) at the average radius \(r = \tfrac{a+b}{2}\) for \(N = 800\), \(I = 1.50\,\text{A}\), \(a = 0.06\,\text{m}\), \(b = 0.08\,\text{m}\).
(b) Calculate the total magnetic energy stored in the toroid’s core volume (assume vacuum).
(c) Conceptual: How does increasing \(a\) while keeping \(b\) fixed affect the field at \(r = \tfrac{a+b}{2}\)?

Problem 7 (Magnetic Dipole Moment & Energy):
The magnetic‑dipole moment of a loop is \(\mu = I\pi r^{2}\).
(a) Calculate \(\mu\) for \(r = 0.10\,\text{m}\), \(I = 4.00\,\text{A}\).
(b) If the loop is placed in a uniform \(0.30\,\text{T}\) field, find the maximum potential energy \(U = \mu B\).
(c) Conceptual: Why is the energy minimum when \(\boldsymbol\mu\) is parallel to \(\mathbf B\)?

Problem 8 (Force Between Parallel Wires):
The force per unit length between identical currents is \(F/L = \dfrac{\mu_0 I^{2}}{2\pi r}\).
(a) Compute \(F/L\) for \(I = 3.00\,\text{A}\) and \(r = 0.05\,\text{m}\).
(b) If the currents flow in opposite directions, is the force attractive or repulsive? Explain briefly.
(c) Conceptual: Summarize how this force underlies the SI definition of the ampere.

Problem 9 (Current Loop in a Non‑Uniform Field):
A square loop (side \(0.10\,\text{m}\), in the \(xy\)-plane) carries \(I = 2.00\,\text{A}\) and sits in \(\mathbf B(x) = B_{0}(1 + \alpha x)\,\hat{\mathbf z}\).
(a) Compute the net force on the loop for \(B_{0} = 0.25\,\text{T}\), \(\alpha = 15\,\text{m}^{-1}\).
(b) Estimate the torque about the loop’s center due to the field gradient and state its direction.
(c) Conceptual: Give one experimental context where such magnetic‑field gradients are important.

Problem 10 (Designing a Hall‑Effect Magnetic Sensor):
(a) List three key design parameters that affect a Hall sensor’s sensitivity and dynamic range.
(b) For a Hall bar 1.0 mm wide, 50 µm thick, carrying 20 mA, and electron density \(n = 8.0\times10^{22}\,\text{m}^{-3}\), estimate the Hall voltage at 0.10 T.
(c) If the supply current doubles, how do the Hall voltage and power dissipation change?
(d) Conceptual: Discuss one trade‑off between increasing sensitivity and managing thermal noise.
