Homework 6: Magnetic Fields

Problems: 10 problems, 3–4 subparts each, varying difficulty

Problem 1 (Lorentz Force Fundamentals): A charged particle moves in uniform, perpendicular electric and magnetic fields.
(a) Write the Lorentz‑force law in vector form and briefly state its physical meaning.
(b) For \(q = 1.00\times10^{-7}\,\text{C}\), \(\mathbf v = 5.00\times10^{3}\,\text{m/s}\,\hat{\mathbf x}\), \(\mathbf B = 0.50\,\text{T}\,\hat{\mathbf z}\), and \(\mathbf E = 0\), calculate \(\mathbf F\).
(c) How would the force change if \(\mathbf v\) were parallel to \(\mathbf B\)? Explain conceptually (no calculation).
(d) If the charge were doubled while all other quantities remained the same, by what factor would \(\mathbf F\) change?

Problem 2 (Uniform Circular Motion in a Magnetic Field): A singly charged ion moves perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field.
(a) Show that its path radius is \(r = \dfrac{mv}{|q|B}\) (one or two lines—no full derivation).
(b) Using \(m = 2.00\times10^{-3}\,\text{kg}\), \(v = 3.00\times10^{3}\,\text{m/s}\), \(|q| = 1.00\times10^{-7}\,\text{C}\), \(B = 0.75\,\text{T}\), compute \(r\).
(c) Determine the cyclotron frequency of the ion.
(d) Conceptual: If \(m\) were halved while \(v,\;q,\;B\) stay the same, what happens to \(r\) and the frequency?

Problem 3 (Field of a Long Straight Wire): Consider an infinite straight wire carrying current \(I\).
(a) State (do not derive) the expression \(B(r) = \dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}\) obtained from Ampère’s law.
(b) Calculate \(B\) at \(r = 0.10\,\text{m}\) for \(I = 5.00\,\text{A}\).
(c) Qualitatively describe how the magnetic‑field lines are oriented around the wire.
(d) If the current is doubled, by what factor does \(B\) at the same \(r\) change?

Problem 4 (Field at the Center of a Circular Loop): A single circular loop of radius \(r\) carries current \(I\).
(a) Write the Biot–Savart–law result \(B = \dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2r}\) for the field at the loop’s center (no derivation).
(b) Evaluate \(B\) for \(r = 0.15\,\text{m}\), \(I = 3.00\,\text{A}\).
(c) If the loop is replaced by \(N = 25\) tightly wound turns (same radius), compute the new field.
(d) Conceptual: Why does increasing \(N\) increase \(B\) linearly rather than quadratically?

Problem 5 (Field Inside a Long Solenoid): A solenoid of length \(L\) has \(n\) turns per unit length and carries current \(I\).
(a) State the result \(B = \mu_0 n I\) (no derivation) and its region of validity.
(b) For \(n = 500\,\text{turns/m}\) and \(I = 2.00\,\text{A}\), compute \(B\).
(c) Determine the magnetic‑energy density \(u = \tfrac{B^{2}}{2\mu_0}\) inside the solenoid.
(d) Conceptual: Explain why the field near the ends deviates from the ideal value.

Problem 6 (Field Inside a Toroid): A toroidal coil has total turns \(N\), carries current \(I\), and has inner radius \(a\) and outer radius \(b\).
(a) Using Ampère’s law, write the expression \(B(r) = \dfrac{\mu_0 N I}{2\pi r}\) for \(a \le r \le b\).
(b) Evaluate \(B\) at the average radius \(r = \tfrac{a+b}{2}\) for \(N = 800\), \(I = 1.50\,\text{A}\), \(a = 0.06\,\text{m}\), \(b = 0.08\,\text{m}\).
(c) Calculate the total magnetic energy stored in the toroid’s core volume (assume vacuum).
(d) Conceptual: How does increasing \(a\) while holding \(b\) fixed affect the field at \(r = \tfrac{a+b}{2}\)?

Problem 7 (Magnetic Dipole Moment): A circular loop of radius \(r\) carries current \(I\).
(a) State \(\mu = I A = I \pi r^{2}\) for the magnetic dipole moment.
(b) Calculate \(\mu\) for \(r = 0.10\,\text{m}\), \(I = 4.00\,\text{A}\).
(c) If the loop is placed in a uniform \(0.30\,\text{T}\) field, find the maximum potential energy \(U = \mu B\).
(d) Conceptual: Why is the energy minimum when \(\boldsymbol\mu\) is parallel to \(\mathbf B\)?

Problem 8 (Force Between Parallel Wires): Two long, straight, parallel wires separated by \(r\) carry equal currents \(I\).
(a) Write the formula for the attraction/repulsion per unit length: \(F/L = \dfrac{\mu_0 I^{2}}{2\pi r}\).
(b) Compute \(F/L\) for \(I = 3.00\,\text{A}\), \(r = 0.05\,\text{m}\).
(c) If the currents flow in opposite directions, state whether the force is attractive or repulsive and why.
(d) Conceptual: Explain how this effect underlies the SI definition of the ampere.

Problem 9 (Non‑Uniform Magnetic Field Effects): A square current loop of side \(0.10\,\text{m}\) lies in the \(xy\)-plane and carries \(I = 2.00\,\text{A}\). It is placed in a field \(\mathbf B(x) = B_{0}(1 + \alpha x)\,\hat{\mathbf z}\).
(a) Write an integral expression for the net force on the loop in terms of \(B_{0},\alpha\).
(b) Evaluate the net force for \(B_{0} = 0.25\,\text{T}\) and \(\alpha = 15\,\text{m}^{-1}\).
(c) Estimate the torque about the loop’s center due to the field gradient.
(d) Conceptual: Identify at least one experimental situation where such gradients are significant.

Problem 10 (Designing a Simple Magnetic Sensor): You are asked to design a Hall‑effect magnetic‑field sensor sensitive from 0 T to 0.10 T.
(a) List three key design parameters that affect sensitivity and dynamic range.
(b) For a Hall bar 1.0 mm wide, 50 µm thick, carrying 20 mA, and with electron density \(n = 8.0\times10^{22}\,\text{m}^{-3}\), estimate the Hall voltage at 0.10 T.
(c) If the supply current were doubled, how would the Hall voltage and power dissipation change?
(d) Conceptual: Discuss one trade‑off between sensitivity and thermal noise in real‑world sensors.
