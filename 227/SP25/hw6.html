Homework 6: Circuits

Problem 1 (Drift Velocity):
A copper wire of radius 1.5 mm carries a steady current of 5.0 A. (a) Calculate the drift velocity of the conduction electrons, given an electron density of \(8.5\times10^{28}\,\mathrm{m}^{-3}\) and the electron charge \(e=1.602\times10^{-19}\,\mathrm{C}\).
(b) Comment briefly on whether this speed is significant compared to everyday velocities.

Problem 2 (Resistance from Resistivity):
A cylindrical rod of aluminum (resistivity \(\rho=2.65\times10^{-8}\,\Omega\!\cdot\!\mathrm{m}\)) has length 2.0 m and diameter 4.0 mm. Calculate its electrical resistance.

Problem 3 (Temperature Dependence of Resistivity):
Copper at 20 °C has resistivity \(\rho_0=1.68\times10^{-8}\,\Omega\!\cdot\!\mathrm{m}\) with temperature coefficient \(\alpha=3.9\times10^{-3}\,^\circ\mathrm{C}^{-1}\). (a) Compute its resistivity at 75 °C.
(b) For a 1.0 m length and cross‑section \(1.0\,\mathrm{mm}^2\), find the percent increase in resistance.
Hint: \(\%\Delta R = \bigl(R_{75^\circ\rm C}-R_{20^\circ\rm C}\bigr)/R_{20^\circ\rm C}\times100\%\).

Problem 4 (Ohm’s Law Check):
A resistor draws 0.20 A at 5.0 V and 0.40 A at 10.0 V. (a) Determine its resistance.
(b) Does this device obey Ohm’s law? Briefly explain, noting how a constant \(V/I\) ratio indicates Ohmic behavior.

Problem 5 (Electrical Power & Energy):
A space heater draws 8.0 A from a 120 V outlet. (a) Calculate its power rating.
(b) If it runs for 2.5 h, how much energy (in kWh) does it consume?

Problem 6 (Mesh Analysis):
(a) Write mesh‐current equations for a two‐loop circuit with a shared resistor (see diagram below). Assume mesh currents \(I_1\) and \(I_2\) both circulate clockwise.
(b) Solve for the branch currents given \[ V_1=12\,\mathrm{V},\quad V_2=5\,\mathrm{V},\quad R_1=100\,\Omega,\quad R_2=150\,\Omega,\quad R_3=200\,\Omega. \]

Problem 7 (Power Rating):
A resistor is rated for a maximum power dissipation of 5.0 W. (a) What is the maximum current it can carry safely if its resistance is 10 Ω?
(b) What is the maximum voltage that can be applied across it without exceeding its power rating?

Problem 8 (Voltage Divider):
Using the voltage‐divider formula, calculate the voltage across \(R_2=200\,\Omega\) when it is in series with \(R_1=100\,\Omega\) on a 12 V source.

Problem 9 (Current Divider):
Using the current‐divider formula, compute the current through each resistor when \(R_1=150\,\Omega\) and \(R_2=300\,\Omega\) share a total of 2 A in parallel.

Problem 10 (RC Circuit Transient Response):
For an RC circuit with \(R=1\,\mathrm{k}\Omega\) and \(C=10\,\mu\mathrm{F}\) on a 12 V source, (a) Determine the time constant \(\tau=RC\).
(b) Calculate the capacitor voltage \(V_C\) at \(t=2\tau\) using \(V_C=V_\infty\bigl(1-e^{-t/\tau}\bigr)\).
