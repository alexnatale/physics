Homework 1 Solutions: Vectors, Electric Charge, and Coulomb's Law

Problem 1: Advanced Vector Operations

(a) Given \(\mathbf{A} = 3\hat{i} - 4\hat{j} + 2\hat{k}\).
Compute its magnitude: \[ |\mathbf{A}| = \sqrt{3^2 + (-4)^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{9+16+4} = \sqrt{29} \approx 5.39. \] Then, the unit vector is: \[ \hat{A} = \frac{\mathbf{A}}{|\mathbf{A}|} \approx 0.56\hat{i} - 0.74\hat{j} + 0.37\hat{k}. \]

(b) For \(\mathbf{B} = \hat{i} + 2\hat{j} - 2\hat{k}\), compute the dot product: \[ \mathbf{A}\cdot\mathbf{B} = 3(1) + (-4)(2) + 2(-2) = 3-8-4 = -9. \] (A negative dot product indicates an obtuse angle between \(\mathbf{A}\) and \(\mathbf{B}\).)

(c) Compute the cross product: \[ \mathbf{A}\times\mathbf{B} = (a_2b_3 - a_3b_2,\; a_3b_1 - a_1b_3,\; a_1b_2 - a_2b_1). \] With the given vectors:
- \(x\): \((-4)(-2) - (2)(2) = 8-4 = 4\),
- \(y\): \((2)(1) - (3)(-2) = 2+6 = 8\),
- \(z\): \((3)(2) - (-4)(1) = 6+4 = 10\).
Thus, \(\mathbf{A}\times\mathbf{B} = 4\hat{i} + 8\hat{j} + 10\hat{k}\).

Problem 2: Vector Addition & Decomposition

(a) Given \(\mathbf{C} = 2\hat{i} + 3\hat{j}\) and \(\mathbf{D} = -\hat{i} + 4\hat{j}\), add them: \[ \mathbf{R} = \mathbf{C} + \mathbf{D} = (2 - 1)\hat{i} + (3 + 4)\hat{j} = \hat{i} + 7\hat{j}. \]

(b) The magnitude is: \[ |\mathbf{R}| = \sqrt{1^2+7^2} = \sqrt{50} \approx 7.07, \] and the direction (angle from the positive \(\hat{i}\)) is: \[ \theta = \arctan\left(\frac{7}{1}\right) \approx 81.87^\circ. \]

(c) The computed magnitude from the components confirms the result in (b).

Problem 3: Projection and Angle Between Vectors

(a) Given \(\mathbf{A} = 4\hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + \hat{k}\) and \(\mathbf{B} = 1\hat{i} + 1\hat{j} + 0\hat{k}\), first compute the dot product: \[ \mathbf{A}\cdot\mathbf{B} = 4(1) + (-2)(1) + 1(0) = 4-2 = 2. \] (Here the “projection” is understood via the dot product value.)

(b) Next, determine the angle using: \[ \cos\theta = \frac{\mathbf{A}\cdot\mathbf{B}}{|\mathbf{A}||\mathbf{B}|}. \] Compute: \[ |\mathbf{A}| = \sqrt{4^2+(-2)^2+1^2} = \sqrt{16+4+1} = \sqrt{21} \approx 4.58, \] \[ |\mathbf{B}| = \sqrt{1^2+1^2} = \sqrt{2} \approx 1.41. \] Then: \[ \cos\theta \approx \frac{2}{4.58 \times 1.41} \approx \frac{2}{6.46} \approx 0.31, \] so \(\theta \approx \arccos(0.31) \approx 72.0^\circ.\)

Problem 4: Resultant of Multiple Vectors

Given:
\(\mathbf{P} = 2\hat{i} + 0\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}\),
\(\mathbf{Q} = -\hat{i} + 4\hat{j} + 0\hat{k}\), and
\(\mathbf{R} = 0\hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + 5\hat{k}\).
Their sum is: \[ \mathbf{S} = \mathbf{P}+\mathbf{Q}+\mathbf{R} = (2-1+0)\hat{i} + (0+4-2)\hat{j} + (3+0+5)\hat{k} = \hat{i}+2\hat{j}+8\hat{k}. \] The magnitude is: \[ |\mathbf{S}| = \sqrt{1^2+2^2+8^2} = \sqrt{1+4+64} = \sqrt{69} \approx 8.31. \]

Problem 5: Cross Product and Area Calculation

(a) Given \(\mathbf{U} = 1\hat{i} + 3\hat{j} - 2\hat{k}\) and \(\mathbf{V} = 4\hat{i} - \hat{j} + 2\hat{k}\), compute: \[ \mathbf{U}\times\mathbf{V} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 1 & 3 & -2 \\ 4 & -1 & 2 \end{vmatrix}. \] Calculation:
- \(x\): \(3\cdot2 - (-2)\cdot(-1) = 6-2 = 4\),
- \(y\): \((-2)\cdot4 - 1\cdot2 = -8-2 = -10\),
- \(z\): \(1\cdot(-1)-3\cdot4 = -1-12 = -13\).
So, \(\mathbf{U}\times\mathbf{V} = 4\hat{i} - 10\hat{j} - 13\hat{k}\).

(b) The area of the parallelogram is: \[ \text{Area} = \sqrt{4^2+(-10)^2+(-13)^2} = \sqrt{16+100+169} = \sqrt{285} \approx 16.88. \]

Problem 6: Coulomb Force Between Two Charges

Given \(q_1 = +2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) and \(q_2 = -3.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) separated by \(r = 0.15\,m\).
Using Coulomb’s law: \[ F = k\frac{|q_1q_2|}{r^2}, \] where \(k \approx 8.99\times10^9\,N\cdot m^2/C^2\).
Here, \[ |q_1q_2| = 2.0\times10^{-7}\times3.0\times10^{-7} = 6.0\times10^{-14}\,C^2,\quad r^2 = (0.15)^2 = 0.0225. \] Thus, \[ F \approx 8.99\times10^9 \times \frac{6.0\times10^{-14}}{0.0225} \approx 0.0240\,N. \] (The force is attractive.)

Problem 7: Superposition of Coulomb Forces

Three charges are aligned along the x‑axis:
\(q_1 = +3.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) at \(x = 0\), \(q_2 = -1.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) at \(x = 0.20\,m\), and \(q_3 = +2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) at \(x = 0.35\,m\).
Force on \(q_2\) due to \(q_1\): \[ F_{12} \approx 8.99\times10^9\times\frac{(3.0\times10^{-7})(1.0\times10^{-7})}{(0.20)^2} \approx 6.74\times10^{-3}\,N, \] acting toward \(q_1\) (negative \(\hat{i}\) direction).
Force on \(q_2\) due to \(q_3\): With separation \(0.35-0.20 = 0.15\,m\): \[ F_{32} \approx 8.99\times10^9\times\frac{(2.0\times10^{-7})(1.0\times10^{-7})}{(0.15)^2} \approx 8.00\times10^{-3}\,N, \] acting toward \(q_3\) (positive \(\hat{i}\) direction).
The net force on \(q_2\) is: \[ F_{net} \approx 8.00\times10^{-3} - 6.74\times10^{-3} = 1.26\times10^{-3}\,N, \] directed to the right.

Problem 8: Effect of Separation on Coulomb Force

Given \(q_1 = +2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) and \(q_2 = -2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) initially separated by \(r = 0.10\,m\).
(a) Initial force: \[ F \approx 8.99\times10^9\times\frac{4.0\times10^{-14}}{(0.10)^2} \approx 0.0360\,N. \] (b) If the separation increases by 20% to \(r = 0.12\,m\): \[ F_{new} \approx 8.99\times10^9\times\frac{4.0\times10^{-14}}{(0.12)^2} \approx 0.0250\,N. \] (c) The force is reduced by approximately \(\left(\frac{0.10}{0.12}\right)^2 \approx 0.69\).

Problem 9: Equilibrium Position for a Test Charge

Given two fixed charges: \(q_1 = +3.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) at \(x = 0\) and \(q_2 = +1.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) at \(x = 0.50\,m\).
A test charge \(q_0 = -1.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) is placed between them. Let its distances from \(q_1\) and \(q_2\) be \(x\) and \(0.50-x\) respectively. For equilibrium: \[ k\frac{|q_0q_1|}{x^2} = k\frac{|q_0q_2|}{(0.50-x)^2}. \] Cancel common factors to get: \[ \frac{3.0\times10^{-7}}{x^2} = \frac{1.0\times10^{-7}}{(0.50-x)^2}. \] This simplifies to: \[ 3(0.50-x)^2 = x^2. \] Taking square roots: \[ \sqrt{3}(0.50-x) = x. \] Solving for \(x\): \[ x = \frac{0.50\sqrt{3}}{1+\sqrt{3}} \approx \frac{0.50\times1.732}{2.732} \approx 0.317\,m. \] Thus, the test charge is about \(0.317\,m\) from \(q_1\) and \(0.183\,m\) from \(q_2\).

Problem 10: Coulomb Force in a Triangular Charge Configuration

Assumptions and Setup:
Consider an equilateral triangle with side length \(L = 0.20\,m\) and vertices at:
- Vertex A at \((0, 0)\), - Vertex B at \((0.20, 0)\), and - Vertex C at \((0.10, 0.1732)\) (since the height \(= 0.20\sqrt{3}/2 \approx 0.1732\,m\)).
The charges are:
\(q_A = +2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) (at A), \(q_B = -2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) (at B), and \(q_C = -2.0\times10^{-7}\,C\) (at C).

Step 1: Force on \(q_A\) due to \(q_B\)
The distance from A to B is \(L = 0.20\,m\). Using Coulomb’s law: \[ F_{AB} = k\frac{|q_Aq_B|}{L^2}, \] with \(|q_Aq_B| = (2.0\times10^{-7})(2.0\times10^{-7}) = 4.0\times10^{-14}\,C^2\) and \(L^2 = 0.04\). Thus, \[ F_{AB} \approx 8.99\times10^9 \times \frac{4.0\times10^{-14}}{0.04} \approx 8.99\times10^{-3}\,N. \] Since \(q_A\) is positive and \(q_B\) is negative, the force is attractive—that is, directed from A toward B (i.e. along the positive \(\hat{i}\)): \[ \vec{F}_{AB} \approx 8.99\times10^{-3}\,N\,\hat{i}. \]

Step 2: Force on \(q_A\) due to \(q_C\)
The distance from A to C is also \(0.20\,m\). Similarly, \[ F_{AC} \approx 8.99\times10^9 \times \frac{4.0\times10^{-14}}{0.04} \approx 8.99\times10^{-3}\,N. \] Since \(q_A\) is positive and \(q_C\) is negative, the force is attractive, directed from A toward C. The vector from A to C is: \[ \vec{r}_{AC} = (0.10,\, 0.1732). \] Its unit vector is: \[ \hat{r}_{AC} = \left(\frac{0.10}{0.20},\, \frac{0.1732}{0.20}\right) = (0.5,\, 0.866). \] Therefore, \[ \vec{F}_{AC} \approx 8.99\times10^{-3}\,N \times (0.5\hat{i} + 0.866\hat{j}) \approx (4.50\times10^{-3}\,\hat{i} + 7.79\times10^{-3}\,\hat{j})\,N. \]

Step 3: Net Force on \(q_A\)
Sum the forces:
\( F_x = 8.99\times10^{-3}\,N + 4.50\times10^{-3}\,N = 13.49\times10^{-3}\,N \),
\( F_y = 0 + 7.79\times10^{-3}\,N = 7.79\times10^{-3}\,N \).
The net force magnitude is: \[ |\vec{F}_{net}| = \sqrt{(13.49\times10^{-3})^2 + (7.79\times10^{-3})^2} \approx \sqrt{0.000182 + 0.000061} \approx \sqrt{0.000243} \approx 0.0156\,N. \] The angle (relative to the +\(\hat{i}\) axis) is: \[ \theta = \arctan\left(\frac{7.79\times10^{-3}}{13.49\times10^{-3}}\right) \approx \arctan(0.577) \approx 30^\circ. \]

Final Answer: The net force on \(q_A\) is approximately \(0.0156\,N\), directed \(30^\circ\) above the +\(\hat{i}\) axis.
