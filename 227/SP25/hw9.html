Homework 9: AC Circuits & Electromagnetic Waves

Due: Friday, May 30, 2025, 11:59 PM
Topics: AC Sources; Simple AC Circuits; Series RLC Circuits; Power & Resonance in AC; Transformers; Maxwell’s Equations; Electromagnetic Waves; Radiation Pressure.
Note: All key equations have been derived and explained in class. Use these equations to complete the problems.

Problem 1 (AC RMS Values):
An AC source is described by \( V(t)=V_0\sin(\omega t) \) with \( V_0 = 170\text{ V} \) and \( \omega = 2\pi(60\text{ Hz}) \). It drives a purely resistive load of \( R = 15\ \Omega \). Using \( V_{\text{rms}}=\dfrac{V_0}{\sqrt{2}},\; I_{\text{rms}}=\dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}}{R},\; P_{\text{avg}} = V_{\text{rms}} I_{\text{rms}} \), calculate:
(a) \( V_{\text{rms}} \)
(b) \( I_{\text{rms}} \)
(c) \( P_{\text{avg}} \)

Problem 2 (Capacitive Reactance):
A 60 Hz source of \( V_{\text{rms}} = 120\text{ V} \) is applied across a capacitor of \( C = 47\ \mu\text{F} \). Using \( X_C = \dfrac{1}{2\pi f C},\; I_{\text{rms}} = \dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}}{X_C},\; I_0 = \sqrt{2}\,I_{\text{rms}} \), calculate:
(a) \( X_C \)
(b) \( I_{\text{rms}} \)
(c) \( I_0 \)

Problem 3 (Inductive Reactance):
A coil with \( L = 120\ \text{mH} \) is connected to a 50 Hz source of \( V_{\text{rms}} = 85\text{ V} \). Using \( X_L = 2\pi f L,\; I_{\text{rms}} = \dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}}{X_L},\; I_0 = \sqrt{2}\,I_{\text{rms}} \), calculate:
(a) \( X_L \)
(b) \( I_{\text{rms}} \)
(c) \( I_0 \)

Problem 4 (Average Power – Resistive Load):
An electric heater with \( R = 50\ \Omega \) is connected to a 230 V rms, 50 Hz outlet. Using \( P_{\text{avg}} = \dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}^{2}}{R},\; E = P_{\text{avg}}\Delta t \), calculate:
(a) \( P_{\text{avg}} \)
(b) The energy \( E \) consumed in \( \Delta t = 3.0\text{ h} \)
(c) The cost at \$0.15 per kWh

Problem 5 (Series RLC Impedance):
A series RLC circuit has \( R = 40\ \Omega,\; L = 80\ \text{mH},\; C = 120\ \mu\text{F} \) and is driven by \( V_{\text{rms}} = 120\text{ V} \) at \( f = 60\text{ Hz} \). Using \( X_L = 2\pi f L,\; X_C = \dfrac{1}{2\pi f C},\; Z = \sqrt{R^{2} + (X_L - X_C)^{2}},\; I_{\text{rms}} = \dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}}{Z},\; \phi = \tan^{-1}\!\bigl(\dfrac{X_L - X_C}{R}\bigr),\; P_{\text{avg}} = V_{\text{rms}} I_{\text{rms}}\cos\phi \), calculate:
(a) \( Z \)
(b) \( I_{\text{rms}} \)
(c) \( \phi \)
(d) \( P_{\text{avg}} \)

Problem 6 (Resonance & Quality Factor):
Using \( R = 40\ \Omega,\; L = 80\ \text{mH},\; C = 120\ \mu\text{F} \):
With \( f_0 = \dfrac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}},\; \Delta f = \dfrac{R}{2\pi L},\; Q = \dfrac{f_0}{\Delta f} \), calculate:
(a) \( f_0 \)
(b) \( \Delta f \)
(c) \( Q \)

Problem 7 (Ideal Transformer):
An ideal transformer has \( N_p = 600 \) primary turns and \( N_s = 120 \) secondary turns. The primary is supplied with 240 V rms. Using \( \dfrac{V_s}{V_p} = \dfrac{N_s}{N_p},\; P_p = P_s,\; P = V_{\text{rms}} I_{\text{rms}} \), calculate:
(a) \( V_s \)
(b) The primary rms current if \( I_s = 4.0\text{ A} \)
(c) The power delivered to the load

Problem 8 (Energy in an EM Wave):
A plane electromagnetic wave in vacuum has \( E_0 = 250\text{ V/m} \). With \( c = 3.00\times10^{8}\text{ m/s},\; \varepsilon_0 = 8.85\times10^{-12}\text{ F/m} \) and \( I_{\text{avg}} = \dfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 c E_0^{2},\; P_{\text{rad}} = \dfrac{I_{\text{avg}}}{c},\; p = P_{\text{rad}} A \Delta t \), calculate:
(a) \( I_{\text{avg}} \)
(b) \( P_{\text{rad}} \) on a perfectly absorbing surface
(c) The momentum delivered to a \( 2.0\text{ m}^{2} \) surface in \( \Delta t = 10\text{ s} \)

Problem 9 (Radiation Pressure & Force):
Inside a microwave oven the average intensity is \( I = 700\text{ W/m}^{2} \). The mesh window has absorbing area \( A = 0.015\text{ m}^{2} \). Using \( P_{\text{rad}} = \dfrac{I}{c},\; F = P_{\text{rad}} A \), calculate:
(a) \( P_{\text{rad}} \)
(b) \( F \)

Problem 10 (Antenna Tuner – RLC & EM Wave):
A series RLC antenna tuner uses \( L = 2.5\ \mu\text{H} \) and a variable \( C \). It is driven by \( V_{\text{rms}} = 30\text{ V} \) at \( f = 7.20\text{ MHz} \); the radiation resistance is \( R = 5.0\ \Omega \). With \( C = \dfrac{1}{(2\pi f)^{2}L},\; I_{\text{rms}} = \dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}}{R}\ \text{(at resonance)},\; P = I_{\text{rms}}^{2} R,\; I_{\text{wave}} = \dfrac{P}{4\pi r^{2}} \), calculate:
(a) \( C \) for resonance
(b) \( I_{\text{rms}} \) at resonance
(c) \( P \) radiated by the antenna
(d) \( I_{\text{wave}} \) at \( r = 1.0\text{ km} \)
