Phys 217 Homework Assignments – Spring 2025 (Revised)

Homework 9: AC Circuits & Electromagnetic Waves

Due: Friday, May 30, 2025, 11:59 PM
Topics: AC sources, simple AC circuits, series-RLC response, power & resonance, transformers, Maxwell’s equations, electromagnetic waves, radiation pressure.
Note: All key equations have been derived and explained in class. Use them—together with the numerical data below—to complete each part.

Problem 1 (AC RMS Values):
A household source delivers \(V(t)=V_0\sin(\omega t)\) with peak voltage \(V_0 = 170\;\text{V}\) at \(f = 60\;\text{Hz}\) to a \(15.0\;\Omega\) resistor.
(a) Calculate the rms voltage.
(b) Determine the rms current through the resistor.
(c) Find the average power dissipated.

Problem 2 (Capacitive Reactance):
A \(47.0\;\mu\text{F}\) capacitor is connected to a 60 Hz supply of \(120\;\text{V}_{\text{rms}}\).
(a) Compute the reactance \(X_C\).
(b) Find the rms current.
(c) What is the peak current?

Problem 3 (Inductive Reactance):
A \(120\;\text{mH}\) inductor is driven by an 85 Vrms, 50 Hz source.
(a) Calculate the reactance \(X_L\).
(b) Determine the rms current.
(c) What is the corresponding peak current?

Problem 4 (Average Power – Resistive Load):
An electric heater with resistance \(R = 50.0\;\Omega\) is connected to a 230 Vrms, 50 Hz outlet.
(a) Determine the average power drawn.
(b) How much electrical energy is used in 3.00 h?
(c) If electricity costs \$0.150 per kWh, what is the cost of running the heater for this time?

Problem 5 (Series-RLC Impedance):
A series circuit contains \(R = 40.0\;\Omega\), \(L = 80.0\;\text{mH}\), and \(C = 120\;\mu\text{F}\). It is driven by \(120\;\text{V}_{\text{rms}}\) at 60 Hz.
(a) Find the impedance \(Z\).
(b) Calculate the rms current.
(c) Determine the phase angle between voltage and current.
(d) What average power is delivered to the circuit?

Problem 6 (Resonance & Quality Factor):
Use the same \(R\), \(L\), and \(C\) values as in Problem 5.
Determine the resonant frequency \(f_0\).

Problem 7 (Ideal Transformer):
An ideal transformer has 600 primary turns and 120 secondary turns. The primary is supplied with 240 Vrms.
(a) What is the secondary rms voltage?
(b) If the secondary current is 4.00 A, find the primary rms current.
(c) Calculate the power delivered to the load.

Problem 8 (Energy in an EM Wave):
A plane electromagnetic wave in vacuum has peak electric-field amplitude \(E_0 = 250\;\text{V/m}\).
(a) Compute its average intensity.
(b) Find the radiation pressure on a perfectly absorbing surface.
(c) How much momentum is delivered to a \(2.00\;\text{m}^2\) absorbing sheet in 10.0 s?

Problem 9 (Radiation Pressure & Force):
A microwave oven cavity produces an average intensity of \(700\;\text{W/m}^2\). The mesh window has an effective absorbing area of \(0.015\;\text{m}^2\).
(a) Determine the radiation pressure on the window.
(b) Calculate the force exerted by the microwaves.

Problem 10 (Antenna Tuner – Combined Concepts):
A series-RLC antenna tuner uses \(L = 2.50\;\mu\text{H}\) and a variable capacitor \(C\). It is driven by \(V_{\text{rms}} = 30.0\;\text{V}\) at \(f = 7.20\;\text{MHz}\). The effective series (radiation) resistance is \(R = 5.00\;\Omega\).
(a) Choose the capacitance \(C\) that brings the circuit to resonance.
(b) Find the rms current at resonance.
(c) Determine the power radiated by the antenna.
(d) Assuming isotropic emission, what average intensity does the wave have 1.00 km from the antenna?
