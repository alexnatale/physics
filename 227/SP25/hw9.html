Phys 217 Homework Assignments – Spring 2025 (Revised)

Homework 9: AC Circuits & Electromagnetic Waves

Due: Friday, May 30, 2025, 11:59 PM

Problem 1 (AC RMS Values):
An AC source is described by \(V(t)=V_0 \sin(\omega t)\), where \(V_0 = 170\text{ V}\) and \(\omega = 2\pi(60\text{ Hz})\). A purely resistive load of \(R = 15\ \Omega\) is connected.
Use the formulas \(V_{\text{rms}}=\dfrac{V_0}{\sqrt{2}},\; I_{\text{rms}}=\dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}}{R},\; P_{\text{avg}} = V_{\text{rms}} I_{\text{rms}}\). Calculate:
(a) \(V_{\text{rms}}\)
(b) \(I_{\text{rms}}\)
(c) The average power delivered to the resistor

Problem 2 (Capacitive Reactance):
A 60 Hz sinusoidal source of \(V_{\text{rms}} = 120\text{ V}\) is applied across a capacitor of \(C = 47\ \mu\text{F}\).
Use the formulas \(X_C=\dfrac{1}{2\pi f C},\; I_{\text{rms}}=\dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}}{X_C},\; I_0 = \sqrt{2}\, I_{\text{rms}}\). Calculate:
(a) The capacitive reactance \(X_C\)
(b) The rms current \(I_{\text{rms}}\)
(c) The peak current \(I_0\)

Problem 3 (Inductive Reactance):
A coil with inductance \(L = 120\ \text{mH}\) is connected to a 50 Hz source of \(V_{\text{rms}} = 85\text{ V}\).
Use the formulas \(X_L = 2\pi f L,\; I_{\text{rms}}=\dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}}{X_L},\; I_0 = \sqrt{2}\, I_{\text{rms}}\). Calculate:
(a) The inductive reactance \(X_L\)
(b) The rms current through the inductor
(c) The peak current

Problem 4 (Average Power in a Resistive Load):
An electric heater with resistance \(R = 50\ \Omega\) is plugged into a 230 V rms, 50 Hz outlet.
Use the formulas \(P_{\text{avg}} = \dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}^{2}}{R},\; E = P_{\text{avg}}\,\Delta t\). Calculate:
(a) The average power dissipated by the heater
(b) The electrical energy consumed in 3.0 h
(c) The cost of this energy at \$0.15 per kWh

Problem 5 (Series RLC Impedance):
A series RLC circuit has \(R = 40\ \Omega,\; L = 80\ \text{mH},\; C = 120\ \mu\text{F}\) and is driven by a sinusoidal source of \(V_{\text{rms}} = 120\text{ V}\) at \(f = 60\text{ Hz}\).
Use the formulas \(X_L = 2\pi f L,\; X_C = \dfrac{1}{2\pi f C},\; Z = \sqrt{R^{2} + (X_L - X_C)^{2}},\; I_{\text{rms}} = \dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}}{Z},\; \phi = \tan^{-1}\!\bigl(\dfrac{X_L - X_C}{R}\bigr),\; P_{\text{avg}} = V_{\text{rms}} I_{\text{rms}} \cos\phi\). Calculate:
(a) The impedance \(Z\)
(b) The rms current \(I_{\text{rms}}\)
(c) The phase angle \(\phi\)
(d) The average power delivered to the circuit

Problem 6 (Resonance and Quality Factor):
Using the same component values as in Problem 5 (\(R = 40\ \Omega,\; L = 80\ \text{mH},\; C = 120\ \mu\text{F}\)):
Use the formulas \(f_0 = \dfrac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}},\; \Delta f = \dfrac{R}{2\pi L},\; Q = \dfrac{f_0}{\Delta f}\). Calculate:
(a) The resonant frequency \(f_0\)
(b) The bandwidth \(\Delta f\)
(c) The quality factor \(Q\)

Problem 7 (Ideal Transformer):
An ideal transformer has \(N_p = 600\) primary turns and \(N_s = 120\) secondary turns. The primary is connected to a 240 V rms source.
Use the relations \(\dfrac{V_s}{V_p} = \dfrac{N_s}{N_p},\; P_p = P_s,\; P = V_{\text{rms}} I_{\text{rms}}\). Calculate:
(a) The secondary rms voltage \(V_s\)
(b) The primary rms current if the secondary current is 4.0 A
(c) The power delivered to the load

Problem 8 (Energy in an Electromagnetic Wave):
A plane electromagnetic wave in vacuum has peak electric field amplitude \(E_0 = 250\text{ V/m}\).
Use the constants \(c = 3.00\times10^8\text{ m/s},\; \varepsilon_0 = 8.85\times10^{-12}\text{ F/m}\).
Use the formulas \(I_{\text{avg}} = \dfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 c E_0^2,\; P_{\text{rad}} = \dfrac{I_{\text{avg}}}{c},\; p = F\,\Delta t = P_{\text{rad}} A \,\Delta t\). Calculate:
(a) The average intensity \(I_{\text{avg}}\)
(b) The radiation pressure on a perfectly absorbing surface
(c) The momentum delivered to a \(2.0\text{ m}^2\) absorbing surface in 10 s

Problem 9 (Radiation Pressure and Force):
The interior of a microwave oven produces an average intensity of \(I = 700\text{ W/m}^2\). The mesh-covered window has an effective absorbing area of \(A = 0.015\text{ m}^2\).
Use the formulas \(P_{\text{rad}} = \dfrac{I}{c},\; F = P_{\text{rad}} A\). Calculate:
(a) The radiation pressure on the window
(b) The resultant force exerted on the window

Problem 10 (Combined RLC and EM Wave):
A series RLC antenna tuner uses an inductance \(L = 2.5\ \mu\text{H}\) and a variable capacitor \(C\). It is driven by a transmitter supplying \(V_{\text{rms}} = 30\text{ V}\) at \(f = 7.20\text{ MHz}\). The radiation resistance (effective series resistance) is \(R = 5.0\ \Omega\).
Use the formulas \(C = \dfrac{1}{(2\pi f)^2 L},\; I_{\text{rms}} = \dfrac{V_{\text{rms}}}{R}\ \text{(at resonance)},\; P = I_{\text{rms}}^{2} R,\; I_{\text{wave}} = \dfrac{P}{4\pi r^{2}}\). Calculate:
(a) The capacitance \(C\) required for resonance
(b) The rms current \(I_{\text{rms}}\) at resonance
(c) The power radiated by the antenna
(d) The average intensity of the emitted electromagnetic wave at a distance of \(r = 1.0\text{ km}\)
